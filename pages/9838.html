Idea

There are at least three different concepts referred to as “the renormalization group”, only the first is really a group:

  1. the Stückelberg-Petermann renormalization group

  2. the Gell-Mann-Low renormalization cocycle?

  3. the rg group of Wilsonian effective field theory

(Brunetti-Dütsch-Fredenhagen 09, p. 10)

For (E,L kin)(E,\mathbf{L}_{kin}) a free Lagrangian field theory on some spacetime with Green hyperbolic equations of motion, the renormalized perturbative S-matrix, in its dependency of a adiabically switched interaction Lagrangian densities gL intg L_{int} to do perturbation theory about, a function on the space ℱ loc\mathcal{F}_{loc} of formal power series in Planck's constant of polynomial local observables, taking values in the Wick algebra of (E,L int)(E,L_{int}):

S L int:ℱ loc⟶𝒲. S_{L_{int}} \;\colon\; \mathcal{F}_{loc} \longrightarrow \mathcal{W} \,.

The Stückelberg-Petermann renormalization group is a group of transformations

ℱ loc ⟶Z ℱ loc L int ↦ Z(L int) \array{ \mathcal{F}_{loc} &\overset{Z}{\longrightarrow}& \mathcal{F}_{loc} \\ \mathbf{L}_{int} &\mapsto& Z(L_{int}) }

on the space of interaction local Lagrangian densities L int\mathbf{L}_{int} such that for S L kinS_{\mathbf{L}_{kin}} and S′ L kinS'_{\mathbf{L}_{kin}} two renormalized perturbative S-matrices perturbing around the same given Green hyperbolic free field theory Lagrangian field theory (E,L int)(E,\mathbf{L}_{int}) (hence two different renormaliation? schemes) there is a unique ZZ relatin them, in that

(1)S(L int)=S′∘Z(L int)) S(\mathbf{L}_{int}) = S' \circ Z(\mathbf{L}_{int}))

for all L intL_{int}. This is the main theorem of perturbative renormalization.

Now it may happen that L kin=L kin(m)\mathbf{L}_{kin} = \mathbf{L}_{kin}(m) depends on a mass parameter and that under scaling transformations on local observables (Dütsch 18, def. 3.19) σ ρ\sigma_\rho we have that with S L kin(m)S_{L_{kin}(m)} a perturbative S-matrix perturbing around L kin(m)L_{kin}(m) that also

σ ρ∘(S L kin(m/ρ))∘σ ρ −1 \sigma_\rho \circ \left(S_{L_{kin}(m/\rho)}\right) \circ \sigma_\rho^{-1}

is a perturbative S-matrix around L kin(m)L_{kin}(m). Therefore in this case the main theorem of perturbative renormalization implies with (1) that there exists a unique transformation Z ρ mZ^m_\rho of the space of interaction Lagrangians such that

σ ρ∘S L kin(m/ρ)∘σ ρ −1(L int)=S L kin(m)(Z ρ m(L int)) \sigma_\rho \circ S_{L_{kin}(m/\rho)} \circ \sigma_\rho^{-1}(\mathbf{L}_{int}) = S_{L_{kin}(m)}(Z^m_\rho(\mathbf{L}_{int}))

for all L int\mathbf{L}_{int}. These Z ρ mZ^m_\rho are the Gell-Mann-Low cocycle? elements. These do not actually form a group, unless m=0m = 0, but satisfy the relation

Z ρ 1ρ 2 m=Z ρ 1 m∘(σ ρ 1∘Z ρ 2 m/ρ 1∘σ ρ 2) Z^m_{\rho_1 \rho_2} \;=\; Z^m_{\rho_1} \circ \left( \sigma_{\rho_1} \circ Z^{m/\rho_1}_{\rho_2} \circ \sigma_{\rho_2} \right)

(Brunetti-Dütsch-Fredenhagen 09 (69), Dütsch 18 (3.325))

Proof

From the definition we have

S L kin(m)∘Z ρ 1ρ 2 m =σ ρ 1∘σ ρ 2∘S L kin(m/ρ 1ρ 2)∘σ ρ 2 −1⏟S L kin(m/ρ 1)∘Z ρ 2 m/ρ 1∘σ ρ 1 −1 =σ ρ 1∘S L kin(m/ρ 1)∘σ ρ 1 −1∘σ ρ 1⏞=id⏟=S L int(m)∘Z ρ 1 m∘σ ρ 1∘Z ρ 2 m/ρ 1∘σ ρ 1 −1 =S L int(m)∘Z ρ 1 m∘σ ρ 1∘Z ρ 2 m/ρ 1∘σ ρ 1 −1 \begin{aligned} S_{L_{kin}(m)} \circ Z^m_{\rho_1 \rho_2} & = \sigma_{\rho_1} \circ \underset{ S_{L_{kin}(m/\rho_1)} \circ Z^{m/\rho_1}_{\rho_2} }{ \underbrace{ \sigma_{\rho_2} \circ S_{L_{kin}(m/\rho_1\rho_2)} \circ \sigma_{\rho_2}^{-1} }} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1}^{-1} \\ & = \underset{= S_{L_{int}(m)} \circ Z^m_{\rho_1} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1}}{ \underbrace{ \sigma_{\rho_1} \circ S_{L_{kin}(m/\rho_1)} \circ \overset{ = id }{ \overbrace{ \sigma_{\rho_1}^{-1} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1} } } }} \circ Z^{m/\rho_1}_{\rho_2} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1}^{-1} \\ & = S_{L_{int}(m)} \circ Z^m_{\rho_1} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1} \circ Z^{m/\rho_1}_{\rho_2} \circ \sigma_{\rho_1}^{-1} \end{aligned}

Now the claim follows with the fact that the perturbative S-matrix S L int(m)S_{L_{int}(m)}, as a function form interaction Lagrangian densities to Wick algebra-elements, is an injective function.
