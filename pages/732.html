Idea

A group TT-complex is ‘’a simplicial T-complex internal to the category of groups’’. They were first studied in Nick Ashley’s thesis (see references below for the published version)

More precisely:

Definition

A group TT-complex is a pair, (G,T)(G,T), in which GG is a simplicial group and TT is a graded subgroup of GG consisting of thin elements, and which satisfies the conditions:

  • Every degenerate element is thin.
  • Every horn in GG has a unique thin filler.
  • A thin filler of a thin box also has its last face thin.

Results

Lemma

Let D=(D n) n≥1D = (D_n)_{n\geq 1} be the graded subgroup of GG generated by the images of the degeneracy maps, s i:G n→G n+1s_i :G_n \rightarrow G_{n+1}, for all ii and nn, then any box in GG has a filler in DD.

Proof

The algorithmic formulae used when proving that any simplicial group is a Kan complex (cf., entry on simplicial group) give a filler defined as a product of degenerate copies of the faces of the given box, so is in D nD_n.

Proposition

If (G,T)(G,T) is a group TT-complex then T=DT = D.

Proof

To see this, we note that as every degenerate element is this, D⊆TD \subseteq T. Conversely if t∈T nt \in T_n, then it fills the box made up of (−,d 1t,…,d nt) ( - , d_1t, \ldots, d_n t ). This, in turn, has a filler, dd, in DD, but, as this filler is also thin, it must be that t=dt = d, since thin fillers are uniquely determined.

This is neat. It says there is basically only one possible group TT-complex structure on a given simplicial group. The next result (again by Ashley) shows that not all simplicial groups carry such a structure.

The group T-complex associated to a simplicial group

This suggests that, given an arbitrary simplicial group, GG, we could form a quotient which would be a group TT-complex, simply by dividing out by the subgroups, NG n∩D nNG_n\cap D_n. We would need check that the face and degeneracy maps worked correctly, that the result did not somehow generate some new ‘thin’ elements, etc. In fact the idea does not work because of a much more elementary problem: NG n∩D nNG_n\cap D_n need not be a normal subgroup of G nG_n!

A variant of this does however work. We need the Conduché non-Abelian version of the decomposition theorem for simplicial groups that makes up an important part of the Dold-Kan correspondence. With that we note the fairly obvious point that when we divide out by a graded subgroup in a simplicial group, then it has effects in all dimensions due to the face and degeneracy maps, so if we kill elements in NG n∩D nNG_n\cap D_n in all dimensions, we must also kill d 0(NG n+1∩D n+1)d_0(NG_{n+1}\cap D_{n+1}) and all the s k(NG n−1∩D n−1)s_k(NG_{n-1}\cap D_{n-1}). The end result of this is a group TT-complex whose Moore complex has a crossed complex structure in a natural way. (The formula is given in the crossed complex entry.)
