Proposition

(canonical momentum)

Consider a Lagrangian field theory (E,L)(E, \mathbf{L}) whose Lagrangian density L\mathbf{L}

  1. does not depend on the spacetime-coordinates

  2. depends on spacetime derivatives of field coordinates (hence on jet bundle coordinates) at most to first order.

Hence if the field bundle E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to} \Sigma is a trivial vector bundle over Minkowski spacetime (example \ref{TrivialVectorBundleAsAFieldBundle}) this means to consider the case that

L=L((ϕ a),(ϕ μ a))dvol Σ. \mathbf{L} \;=\; L\left( (\phi^a), (\phi^a_{\mu}) \right) dvol_\Sigma \,.

Then the presymplectic current (def. \ref{EulerLagrangeOperatorForTivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) is (up to possible a horizontally exact part) of the form

Ω BFV=δp a μ∧δϕ a∧ι ∂ μdvol Σ \Omega_{BFV} \;=\; \delta p_a^\mu \wedge \delta \phi^a \wedge \iota_{\partial_\mu} dvol_\Sigma

where

p a μ≔∂L∂ϕ ,μ a. p_a^\mu \;\coloneqq\; \frac{\partial L}{ \partial \phi^a_{,\mu}} \,.

Here p a≔p a 0p_a \coloneqq p_a^0 is called the “canonical momentum” corresponding to the “canonical field coordinate” ϕ a\phi^a.

In the language of multisymplectic geometry the full expression

p a μι ∂ μdvol Σ∈Ω Σ p,1(E) p_a^\mu \iota_{\partial_\mu} dvol_\Sigma \;\in\; \Omega^{p,1}_\Sigma(E)

is also called the “canonical multi-momentum”.

Proof

We compute:

dL=(∂L∂ϕ aδϕ a+∂L∂ϕ ,μ aδϕ ,μ a)dvol Σ =(∂L∂ϕ a−ddx μ∂L∂ϕ ,μ a)dvol Σ+d(∂L∂ϕ ,μ aδϕ a)ι ∂ μdvol Σ \begin{aligned} \mathbf{d} \mathbf{L} \;=\; \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \phi^a} \delta \phi^a + \frac{\partial L}{\partial \phi^a_{,\mu}} \delta \phi^a_{,\mu} \right) dvol_\Sigma & = \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \phi^a} - \frac{d}{d x^\mu} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \phi^a_{,\mu}} \right) dvol_\Sigma + d \left( \frac{\partial L}{\partial \phi^a_{,\mu}} \delta \phi^a \right) \iota_{\partial_\mu} dvol_\Sigma \end{aligned}
