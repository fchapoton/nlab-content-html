Proof

Let {p ρ n+1} k∈ℕ\{p_{\rho_{n+1}}\}_{k \in \mathbb{N}} be a sequence of projection maps as in (eq:ForExtensionOfDistributionsProjectionMaps) defining an Epstein-Glaser ("re"-)normalization (prop. \ref{RenormalizationIsInductivelyExtensionToDiagonal}) of time-ordered products {T k} k∈ℕ\{T_k\}_{k \in \mathbb{N}} as extensions of distributions of the T kT_k, regarded as distributions via remark \ref{TimeOrderedProductOfFixedInteraction}, by the choice q k α=0q_k^\alpha = 0 in (eq:ExtensionOfDitstributionsPointFixedAndChoice).

We will construct that 𝒵 Λ\mathcal{Z}_\Lambda in terms of these projections p ρp_\rho.

First consider some convenient shorthand:

For n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}, write 𝒵 ≤n≔∑1∈{1,⋯,n}1n!Z n\mathcal{Z}_{\leq n} \coloneqq \underset{1 \in \{1, \cdots, n\}}{\sum} \frac{1}{n!} Z_n. Moreover, for k∈ℕk \in \mathbb{N} write (T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n) k(T_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n})_k for the kk-ary coefficient in the expansion of the composite 𝒮 Λ∘𝒵 ≤n\mathcal{S}_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n}, as in equation (eq:MainTheoremPerturbativeRenormalizationInductionStep) in the proof of the main theorem of perturbative renormalization (prop. \ref{AnyTwoSMatrixRenormalizationSchemesDifferByAUniqueVertexRedefinition}).

In this notation we need to find 𝒵 Λ\mathcal{Z}_\Lambda such that for each n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N} we have

(1)limΛ→∞(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) n=T n. \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \left( T_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda} \right)_n \;=\; T_n \,.

We proceed by induction over n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}.

Since by definition T 0=const 1T_0 = const_1, T 1=idT_1 = id and Z 0=const 0Z_0 = const_0, Z 1=idZ_1 = id the statement is trivially true for n=0n = 0 and n=1n = 1.

So assume now that for n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N} that {Z k} k≤n\{Z_{k}\}_{k \leq n} has been found such that (1) holds.

Observe that with the chosen renormalizing projection p ρ n+1p_{\rho_{n+1}} the time-ordered product T n+1T_{n+1} may be expressed as follows:

(2)T n+1(O,⋯,O) =⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(T |I|(I))⋆ F(T |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ k(O⊗⋯⊗O)⟩ =⟨limΛ→∞∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(T |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ(T |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ k(O⊗⋯⊗O)⟩. \begin{aligned} T_{n+1}(O, \cdots, O) & = \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F} \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_k}(O \otimes \cdots \otimes O) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_k}(O \otimes \cdots \otimes O) \right\rangle \end{aligned} \,.

Here in the first step we inserted the decomposition (eq:TimeOrderedProductsAwayFromDiagonalByInduction) of T n+1T_{n+1} in terms of the {T k} k≤n\{T_k\}_{k \leq n} away from the diagonal, as in the proof of prop. \ref{RenormalizationIsInductivelyExtensionToDiagonal}, which is admissible because the image of p ρ n+1p_{\rho_{n+1}} vanishes on the diagonal. In the second step we replaced the star-product of the Feynman propagator Δ F\Delta_F with the limit over the star-products of the regularized propagators Δ F,Λ\Delta_{F,\Lambda}, which converges by the nature of the Hörmander topology.

Hence it is sufficient to find Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda} and K n+1,ΛK_{n+1,\Lambda} such that we have

(3)⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 Λ) n+1,(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(T |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ(T |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ k(−,⋯,−)⟩ =+K k,Λ(−,⋯,−) \begin{aligned} \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\Lambda} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, (-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle & = \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_{k}}\left( -, \cdots, - \right) \right\rangle \\ & \phantom{=} + K_{k,\Lambda}(-, \cdots, -) \end{aligned}

subject to these two conditions:

  1. 𝒵 Λ\mathcal{Z}_\Lambda is local;

  2. limΛ→∞K Λ,k=0\underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} K_{\Lambda,k} = 0.

Now by expanding out the left hand side of (3) as

(T Λ∘𝒵 Λ) n+1=Z n+1,Λ+(T Λ∘Z ≤n,Λ) n+1 (T_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_\Lambda)_{n+1} \;=\; Z_{n+1,\Lambda} \;+\; (T_\Lambda \circ Z_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{n+1}

(which uses the condition T 1=idT_1 = id) we find the unique solution of (3) for Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda}, in terms of the {Z ≤n,Λ}\{Z_{\leq n,\Lambda}\} and K n+1,ΛK_{n+1,\Lambda} (the latter still to be chosen) to be:

(4)⟨Z n+1,Λ,(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(T |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ(T |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =−⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) n+1,(−,⋯,−)⟩ =+⟨K n+1,Λ,(−,⋯,−)⟩. \begin{aligned} \left\langle Z_{n+1,\Lambda} , (-,\cdots, -) \right\rangle & = \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \\ & \phantom{=} - \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n,\Lambda} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, (-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \\ & \phantom{=} + \left\langle K_{n+1, \Lambda}, (-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \end{aligned} \,.

We claim that the following choice works:

(5)K n+1,Λ(−,⋯,−) ≔⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) n+1,p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =−⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(T |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ(T |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩. \begin{aligned} K_{n+1, \Lambda}(-, \cdots, -) & \coloneqq \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \\ & \phantom{=} - \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \end{aligned} \,.

To prove this, we need to show that 1) the resulting Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda} is local and 2) the limit of K n+1,ΛK_{n+1,\Lambda} vanishes as Λ→∞\Lambda \to \infty.

First regarding the locality of Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda}: By inserting (5) into (4) we obtain

⟨Z n+1,Λ,(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n) n+1,p(−,⋯,−)⟩−⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n) n+1,(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n) n+1,(p ρ n+1−id)(−,⋯,−)⟩ \begin{aligned} \left\langle Z_{n+1,\Lambda} \,,\, (-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle & = \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, p(-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle - \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, (-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \left( T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n} \right)_{n+1} \,,\, ( p_{\rho_{n+1}} - id)(-, \cdots, -) \right\rangle \end{aligned}

By definition p ρ n+1−idp_{\rho_{n+1}} - id is the identity on test functions (adiabatic switchings) that vanish at the diagonal. This means that Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda} is supported on the diagonal, and is hence local.

Second we need to show that limΛ→∞K n+1,Λ=0\underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} K_{n+1,\Lambda} = 0:

By applying the analogous causal decomposition (eq:TimeOrderedProductsAwayFromDiagonalByInduction) to the regularized products, we find

(6) ⟨(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) n+1,p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)((T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ((T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩. \begin{aligned} & \left\langle (T_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{n+1} \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \,. \end{aligned}

Using this we compute as follows:

(7) ⟨limΛ→∞(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) n+1,p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨limΛ→∞∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)((T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I|(I))⋆ F,Λ((T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I¯|(I¯)),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)(limΛ→∞(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I|(I))⏟T |I|(I)⋆ F,Λ(limΛ→∞(T Λ∘𝒵 ≤n,Λ) |I¯|(I¯))⏟T |I¯|(I¯),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩ =⟨limΛ→∞∑I∈{1,⋯,n+1}I,I¯≠∅χ i(X)T |I|(I)⋆ F,ΛT |I¯|(I¯),p ρ n+1(−,⋯,−)⟩. \begin{aligned} & \left\langle \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} (T_\Lambda \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{n+1} \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { \mathbf{I}, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \!\!\chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \left( {\, \atop \,} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) {\, \atop \,} \right) \star_{F,\Lambda} \left( {\, \atop \,} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) {\, \atop \,} \right) \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { I, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, \underset{ T_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}}(\mathbf{I}) }{ \underbrace{ \left( \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{{\vert \mathbf{I}\vert}} (\mathbf{I}) \right) }} \star_{F,\Lambda} \underset{ T_{{\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}}\vert}}(\overline{\mathbf{I}}) }{ \underbrace{ \left( \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} (T_{\Lambda} \circ \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda})_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} } ( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) \right) }} \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \\ & = \left\langle \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \underset{ {\mathbf{I} \in \{1, \cdots, n+1\} } \atop { I, \overline{\mathbf{I}} \neq \emptyset } }{\sum} \chi_i(\mathbf{X})\, T_{ { \vert \mathbf{I} \vert } }( \mathbf{I} ) \star_{F,\Lambda} T_{ {\vert \overline{\mathbf{I}} \vert} }( \overline{\mathbf{I}} ) \,,\, p_{\rho_{n+1}}(-,\cdots,-) \right\rangle \end{aligned} \,.

Here in the first step we inserted (6); in the second step we used that in the Hörmander topology the product of distributions preserves limits in each variable and under the braces we used the induction assumption (1) and the definition of UV cutoff (def. \ref{CutoffsUVForPerturbativeQFT}); in the third step we used that by the causal nature of χ i\chi_i (eq:PartitionCausalOfUnityForComplementOfDiagonal) the two factors of the star product are in causal order so that we may equivalently use the Wick algebra-product ⋆ H\star_H (by this prop.); in the fourth step we applied (eq:TimeOrderedProductsAwayFromDiagonalByInduction); and finally we rewrote as in (2), for emphasis.

Inserting this for the left summand in (5) makes manifest that limΛ→∞K n+1,Λ=0\underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} K_{n+1, \Lambda} = 0.

In conclusion this shows that a consistent choice of counterterms 𝒵 Λ\mathcal{Z}_\Lambda exists. To see that every S-matrix scheme arises this way from a choice of 𝒵 Λ\mathcal{Z}_\Lambda it is now sufficient, by the above proof, to see that every S-matrix scheme comes from vanishing renormalization constants q k αq^\alpha_k in (eq:ExtensionOfDitstributionsPointFixedAndChoice) for some sequence of projections {p ρ k} k∈ℕ\{p_{\rho_k}\}_{k \in \mathbb{N}} (in the notation of the proof of prop. \ref{SpaceOfPointExtensions}). This is pretty clear…
