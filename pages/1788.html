Let {p ρ k} k∈ℕ\{p_{\rho_k}\}_{k \in \mathbb{N}} be a sequence of projection maps as in this equation defining an Epstein-Glaser ("re"-)normalization of time-ordered products {T k} k∈ℕ\{T_k\}_{k \in \mathbb{N}} by the choice q k α=0q_k^\alpha = 0 in this equation.

This means that we may write the corresponding S-matrix as

𝒮=limΛ→∞(∑k∈ℕ1k!1(iℏ k)((−)⋆ F,Λ⋯⋆ F,Λ(−)⏟karguments)∘p ρ k) \mathcal{S} \;=\; \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \left( \underset{k \in \mathbb{N}}{\sum} \frac{1}{k!} \frac{1}{(i \hbar^k)} \left( \underset{ k \, \text{arguments} }{ \underbrace{ (-) \star_{F,\Lambda} \cdots \star_{F,\Lambda} (-) } } \right) \circ p_{\rho_k} \right)

because, as in the proof of this prop., with the projection p ρ kp_{\rho_k} included, the extension of distributions to the diagonal is unique.

By definition we have Z 0,Λ=0Z_{0,\Lambda} = 0 and Z 1,Λ=idZ_{1,\Lambda} = id. So assume {Z k,Λ} k≤n\{Z_{k,\Lambda}\}_{k \leq n} has been found for some n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}.

We may solve the required equation equivalently for the corresponding effective actions S eff=S eff,0,∞S_{eff} = S_{eff,0,\infty} (using the notation rom this def.), given by the Feynman perturbation series over connected graphs (by this prop.), since that uniquelydetermines the genuine S-matrices by exponentiation with respect to the pointwise product via

𝒮(O)=exp ⋅(S eff,0,∞(O))AAA𝒮 Λ(O)=exp ⋅(S eff,0,Λ(O)) \mathcal{S}(O) = \exp_\cdot( S_{eff,0,\infty}(O) ) \phantom{AAA} \mathcal{S}_\Lambda(O) = \exp_\cdot( S_{eff,0,\Lambda}(O) )

Hence for any local observable

O∈LocObs(E BV-BRST)[[ℏ,g,j]]⟨g,j⟩ O \;\in\; LocObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})[ [ \hbar, g, j] ]\langle g, j\rangle

the equation to be solved is

S eff,0,∞(O)=limΛ→∞𝒮 eff,0,Λ(𝒵 Λ(O)) S_{eff,0,\infty}(O) \;=\; \underset{\Lambda \to \infty }{\lim} \mathcal{S}_{eff,0,\Lambda} \left( \mathcal{Z}_\Lambda(O) \right)

By induction assumption may then, as before, write also the renormalized effective as a limit over Λ\Lambda, now with the vertex redefinitions 𝒵 ≤n\mathcal{Z}_{\leq n} included, if we still keep the p ρp_{\rho}-projections:

S eff,0,∞=limΛ→∞∑Γ∈𝒢 connΓ Λ∘p ρ(𝒵 ≤n(−),⋯,𝒵 ≤n(−))\.− S_{eff,0,\infty} \;=\; \underset{\Lambda \to \infty}{\lim} \underset{\Gamma\in \mathcal{G}_{conn}}{\sum} \Gamma_\Lambda \circ p_{\rho} \left( \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n}(-) , \cdots , \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n}(-) \right) \.-

Here Γ Λ\Gamma_\Lambda denotes the Feynman amplitude computed with ⋆ F,Λ\star_{F,\Lambda}.

By inserting a sum of observables ∑1≤i≤n+1κ iO i\underset{1 \leq i \leq {n+1}}{\sum} \kappa_i O_i and then differentiating at κ 1,⋯,κ n+1=0\kappa_1, \cdots, \kappa_{n+1} = 0 we may solve for Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda}:

Z n+1,Λ(O 1,⋯,O n+1) =∑Γ∈𝒢 conn(Γ Λ∘p ρ n+1(𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O 1),⋯,𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O n+1))−Γ Λ(𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O 1),⋯,𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O n+1))) =∑Γ∈𝒢 connΓ Λ∘(p ρ−id)(𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O 1),⋯,𝒵 ≤n,Λ(O n+1)) \begin{aligned} Z_{n+1, \Lambda}(O_1, \cdots, O_{n+1}) & = \underset{ \Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{conn} }{\sum} \left( \Gamma_{\Lambda} \circ p_{\rho_{n+1}} \left( \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n,\Lambda}(O_1), \cdots, \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda}(O_{n+1}) \right) - \Gamma_{\Lambda} \left( \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n,\Lambda}(O_1), \cdots, \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda }(O_{n+1}) \right) \right) \\ & = \underset{ \Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{conn} }{\sum} \Gamma_\Lambda \circ ( p_{\rho} - id ) \left( \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n,\Lambda}(O_1), \cdots, \mathcal{Z}_{\leq n, \Lambda }(O_{n+1}) \right) \end{aligned}

For this to be consistent, we need to show that Z n+1,ΛZ_{n+1,\Lambda} thus defined is local. This follows since the difference

p ρ n+1−id p_{\rho_{n+1}} - id

vanishes on test functions (adiabatic switching) which are supported away from the diagonal, becuase by the fact that p ρp_\rho projects onto the space of functions that vanish at the diagonal, together with their partial derivatives, to some order, it is the identity on these functions.
