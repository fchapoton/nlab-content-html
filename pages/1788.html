Let (E,L)(E, \mathbf{L}) be a Lagrangian field theory. Assume for simplicity of exposition that the field bundle

E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to}\Sigma is a trivial vector bundle over Minkowski spacetime and that (ϕ a) a=1 s(\phi^a)_{a = 1}^s is a linear basis for its typical fiber. This means that the jet bundle J Σ ∞(E)J^\infty_\Sigma(E) has canonical coordinates given by

((x μ),(ϕ a),(ϕ ,μ a),(ϕ ,μ 1μ 2 a),⋯). \left( (x^\mu), (\phi^a), (\phi^a_{,\mu}), (\phi^a_{,\mu_1 \mu_2}), \cdots \right) \,.

Here the ϕ a\phi^a are also called the field coordinates.

Definition

(evolutionary vector fields and antifields)

Let E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to} \Sigma be a fiber bundle. Then an evolutionary vector field vv is a smooth bundle morphism

J Σ ∞(E) ⟶v V ΣE jb ∞,0↘ ↙ E. \array{ J^\infty_\Sigma(E) && \overset{v}{\longrightarrow} && V_\Sigma E \\ & {}_{\mathllap{jb_{\infty,0}}}\searrow && \swarrow_{\mathllap{}} \\ && E } \,.

We write

Γ Σ ev(TJ Σ ∞(E))∈Ω Σ 0,0(E)Mod ℤ \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(T J^\infty_\Sigma(E)) \;\in\; \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) Mod^{\mathbb{Z}}

for the space of evolutionary vector fields, regarded as a graded module over the ℝ\mathbb{R}-algebra

Ω Σ 0,0(E)=C ∞(J Σ ∞(E)) \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \;=\; C^\infty\left( J^\infty_\Sigma(E) \right)

of smooth functions on the jet bundle.

For the construction of the BV-BRST complex in the following, we will want to assign a degree to evolutionary vector field, namely degree -1. The above module regarded as a graded module concentrated all in degree -1 is denoted by

Γ Σ ev(TJ Σ ∞(E))[−1]∈Ω Σ 0,0(E)Mod ℤℤ. \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(T J^\infty_\Sigma(E))[-1] \;\in\; \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) Mod^{\mathbb{Z}}^{\mathbb{Z}} \,.

In the special case that E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to} \Sigma is a trivial vector bundle with field coordinates (ϕ a) a=1 s(\phi^a)_{a = 1}^s as in example \ref{TrivialVectorBundleAsAFieldBundle}, then we write

ϕ¯ a≔∂ ϕ a[−1] \overline \phi_a \;\coloneqq\; \partial_{\phi^a}[-1]

for the induced basis elements of the space of those vector fields on the jet bundle (def. \ref{JetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) which are dual to the field coordinates, but regarded in degree -1. These then provide a linear basis for the space of evolutionary vector fields in that the canonical linear map is an isomorphism of graded modules:

(1)Ω Σ 0,0(E)⟨ϕ¯ a⟩⟶≃Γ Σ ev(V Σ(E))[−1]. \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \langle \overline{\phi}_a \rangle \overset{\simeq}{\longrightarrow} \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma(E))[-1] \,.

These generators ϕ¯ a\overline{\phi}_a in degree -1 are called the antifield coordinates corresponding to the field? coordinates ϕ a\phi^a.

Definition

(implicit infinitesimal gauge symmetry)

Given a Lagrangian field theory (E,L)(E, \mathbf{L}) (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}), then an implicit infinitesimal gauge symmetry is an evolutionary vector field ϵ∈Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)\epsilon \in \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E) on which the Euler-Lagrange variational derivative (prop. \ref{EulerLagrangeOperatorForTivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) vanishes:

ι ϵδ ELL=0. \iota_\epsilon \delta_{EL}\mathbf{L} = 0 \,.

In the special case that the field bundle E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to} \Sigma is a trivial vector bundle over Minkowski spacetime (example \ref{TrivialVectorBundleAsAFieldBundle}) with field coordinates (ϕ a)(\phi^a) and (ϕ¯ a)(\overline{\phi}_a) and antifield coordinates ϕ¯ a\overline{\phi}_a (1) an evolutionary vector field has an expansion of the form

ϵ=R a∂ ϕ a \epsilon = R^a \partial_{\phi^a}

where (R a∈Ω Σ 0,0(E)) a=1 s(R^a \in \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E))_{a = 1}^s are smooth functions on the jet bundle; and in terms of these coordinates the condition on ϵ\epsilon to be a gauge symmetryis equivalent to the condition

R aδ ELLδϕ a=0AAA∈Ω Σ 0,0(E). R^a \frac{\delta_{EL} L}{ \delta \phi^a} = 0 \phantom{AAA} \in \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \,.
Example

(trivial implicial infinitesimal gauge transformations)

Let (E,L)(E,\mathbf{L}) be a Lagrangian field theory (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}).

Then every evolutionary vector field (def. 1) of the special form

ϵ≔δ ELLδϕ aκ [ab]∂ ϕ a∈Γ Σ ev(V ΣE) \epsilon \coloneqq \frac{\delta_{EL} L }{\delta \phi^a} \kappa^{[a b]} \partial_{\phi^a} \;\in\; \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)

for a collection of smooth functions (κ [ab]∈Ω Σ 0,0(E))(\kappa^{[a b]} \in \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)) which is skew-symmetric in its indices (κ [ab]=−κ [ba]\kappa^{[a b]} = - \kappa^{[b a]}) is an implicit infinitesimal gauge symmetry (def. 2).

But since this is so for a “trivial reason” namely just that skew symmetry:

ι ϵδ ELL=undeerset=0(δ ELLδϕ a)(δ ELLδϕ b)κ [ab]⏟dvol Σ \iota_\epsilon \delta_{EL} \mathbf{L} = \undeerset{= 0}{ \underbrace{ \left( \frac{\delta_{EL} L }{\delta \phi^a} \right) \left( \frac{\delta_{EL} L }{\delta \phi^b} \right) \kappa^{[a b]} } } \, dvol_\Sigma

these are called the trivial implicit infinitesimal gauge transformations.

Proposition

For (E,L)(E, \mathbf{L}) a Lagrangian field theory (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}), then every implicit infinitesimal gauge symmetry ϵ\epsilon (def. 2) is in particular an infinitesimal symmetry of the Lagrangian (def. \ref{SymmetriesAndConservedCurrents}) in that

ι ϵδL=dJ˜ ϵ \iota_\epsilon \delta \mathbf{L} \;=\; d \tilde J_\epsilon

for some J˜ ϵ\tilde J_\epsilon. Moreover, the conserved currents associated to such ϵ\epsilon by Noether's theorem (prop. \ref{NoethersFirstTheorem}) vanish identically.

Proof

This follows directly from the definitions: Using δL=δ ELL+dΘ\delta \mathbf{L} = \delta_{EL} \mathbf{L} + d \Theta (prop. \ref{EulerLagrangeOperatorForTivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) and the defining property ι ϵδ ELL=0\iota_\epsilon \delta_{EL}\mathbf{L} = 0 (def. 2) together with the verticality of ϵ\epsilon (ι ϵ∘d=−d∘ι ϵ\iota_{\epsilon} \circ d = - d \circ \iota_{\epsilon}) we have:

ι ϵδL =ι ϵ(δ ELL−dΘ) =ι ϵδ ELL⏟=0−ι ϵdΘ⏟=−dι ϵΘ =dι ϵΘ⏟=J˜ ϵ \begin{aligned} \iota_\epsilon \delta \mathbf{L} & = \iota_\epsilon \left( \delta_{EL} \mathbf{L} - d \Theta \right) \\ & = \underset{ = 0 }{ \underbrace{ \iota_\epsilon \delta_{EL}\mathbf{L} } } - \underset{ = - d \iota_\epsilon \Theta}{ \underbrace{ \iota_\epsilon d \Theta } } \\ & = d \underset{ = \tilde J_\epsilon }{\underbrace{ \iota_\epsilon \Theta }} \end{aligned}
Definition

(BV-complex of ordinary Lagrangian density)

Let (E,L)(E,\mathbf{L}) be a Lagrangian field theory (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime})

Evaluation of evolutionary vector fields (def. 1) in the Euler-Lagrange variational derivative δ ELL∈Ω Σ p,0(E)∧δΩ Σ 0,0(E)\delta_{EL} \mathbf{L} \in \Omega^{p,0}_\Sigma(E) \wedge \delta \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) (prop. \ref{EulerLagrangeOperatorForTivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) yields a Ω Σ 0,0(E)\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)-linear map (of degree +1 if we consider the evolutionary vector fields in degree -1)

ι δ ELL:Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]⟶Ω Σ p+1,0(E). \iota_{\delta_{EL}\mathbf{L}} \;\colon\; \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \longrightarrow \Omega^{p+1,0}_\Sigma(E) \,.

If we use the volume form dvol Σdvol_\Sigma on spacetime Σ\Sigma to induce an identification

Ω Σ p+1,0(E)≃C ∞(J Σ ∞(E))⟨dvol σ⟩ \Omega^{p+1,0}_\Sigma(E) \;\simeq\; C^\infty(J^\infty_\Sigma(E))\langle dvol_\sigma\rangle

with respect to which the Lagrangian density decomposes as

L=Ldvol Σ \mathbf{L} = L dvol_\Sigma

then this is a linear map of the form

ι δ ELL:Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]⟶Ω Σ 0,0(E). \iota_{\delta_{EL} L} \;\colon\; \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \longrightarrow \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \,.

In the special case that the field bundle E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to} \Sigma is a trrivial vector bundle? (example \ref{TrivialVectorBundleAsAFieldBundle}) with field coordinates (ϕ a)(\phi^a) so that the Euler-Lagrange variational derivative has the expansion

δ ELL=δ ELLδϕ aδϕ a \delta_{EL}L \;=\; \frac{\delta_{EL} L}{\delta \phi^a} \delta \phi^a

then this map is given on the antifield basis elements (1) by

ι δ ELL:ϕ¯ a↦δ ELLδϕ a. \iota_{\delta_{EL}L} \;\colon\; \overline{\phi}_a \;\mapsto\; \frac{\delta_{EL} L}{\delta \phi^a} \,.

Consider then the graded symmetric algebra

Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]) Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right)

which is generated over Ω Σ 0,0(E)\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) from the evolutionary vector fields in degree -1, and let

δ BV:Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1])⟶Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]) \delta_{BV} \;\colon\; Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right) \;\longrightarrow\; Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right)

be the unique extension of the linear map ι δ ELL\iota_{\delta_{EL}L} to a derivation of degree +1 on this algebra.

The resulting differential graded-commutative algebra

(Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]),δ BV) \left( Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right) \,,\, \delta_{BV} \right)

is called the BV-complex of the Lagrangian field theory.

is functions on the shell.

is the trivial implicit infinitesimal gauge transformations (example 1).
