Let (E,L)(E, \mathbf{L}) be a Lagrangian field theory. Assume for simplicity of exposition that the field bundle E→fbΣE \overset{fb}{\to}\Sigma is a trivial vector bundle over Minkowski spacetime and that (ϕ a) a=1 s(\phi^a)_{a = 1}^s is a linear basis for its typical fiber. This means that the jet bundle J Σ ∞(E)J^\infty_\Sigma(E) has canonical coordinates given by

((x μ),(ϕ a),(ϕ ,μ a),(ϕ ,μ 1μ 2 a),⋯). \left( (x^\mu), (\phi^a), (\phi^a_{,\mu}), (\phi^a_{,\mu_1 \mu_2}), \cdots \right) \,.

Here the ϕ a\phi^a are also called the field coordinates.

Consider then the space of evolutionary vector fields on the jet bundle

Γ Σ ev(TJ Σ ∞(E))[−1]∈Ω Σ 0,0(E)Mod ℤ \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(T J^\infty_\Sigma(E))[-1] \;\in\; \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) Mod^{\mathbb{Z}}

regarded as a graded module, concentrated in degree -1, over the ℝ\mathbb{R}-algebra

Ω Σ 0,0(E)=C ∞(J Σ ∞(E)) \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \;=\; C^\infty\left( J^\infty_\Sigma(E) \right)

of smooth functions on the jet bundle.

If

ϕ¯ a≔∂ ϕ a[−1] \overline \phi_a \;\coloneqq\; \partial_{\phi^a}[-1]

denotes the canonical basis elements of the space of vector fields on the jet bundle which are dual to the field coordinates, and regarded in degree -1, then by (…) these span the space of evolutionary vector fields in that we have an isomorphism of graded modules of the form

Γ Σ ev(V Σ(E))[−1]≃Ω Σ 0,0(E)⟨ϕ¯ a⟩. \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma(E))[-1] \;\simeq\; \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \langle \overline{\phi}_a \rangle \,.

Evaluation of evolutionary vector fields in the Euler-Lagrange variational derivative δ ELL∈Ω Σ p,0(E)∧δΩ Σ 0,0(E)\delta_{EL} \mathbf{L} \in \Omega^{p,0}_\Sigma(E) \wedge \delta \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) yields a Ω Σ 0,0(E)\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)-linear map of degree +1

ι δ ELL:Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]⟶Ω Σ p+1,0(E). \iota_{\delta_{EL}\mathbf{L}} \;\colon\; \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \longrightarrow \Omega^{p+1,0}_\Sigma(E) \,.

If we use the volume form dvol Σdvol_\Sigma on spacetime Σ\Sigma, regarded as an a horizontal p+1p+1-form on the jet bundle, to induce an identification

Ω Σ p+1,0(E)≃C ∞(J Σ ∞(E))⟨dvol σ⟩ \Omega^{p+1,0}_\Sigma(E) \;\simeq\; C^\infty(J^\infty_\Sigma(E))\langle dvol_\sigma\rangle

with respect to which the Lagrangian density decomposes as

L=Ldvol Σ \mathbf{L} = L dvol_\Sigma

then this is a linear map of the form

ι δ ELL:Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]⟶Ω Σ 0,0(E). \iota_{\delta_{EL} L} \;\colon\; \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \longrightarrow \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) \,.

Moreover if we use the given canonical coordinates, in which the Euler-Lagrange form is expanded as

δ ELL/dvol Σ=δ ELL=δ ELLδϕ aδϕ a \delta_{EL}\mathbf{L}/dvol_\Sigma = \delta_{EL}L \;=\; \frac{\delta_{EL} L}{\delta \phi^a} \delta \phi^a

then the components of this map are

ι δ ELL:ϕ¯ a↦δ ELLδϕ a. \iota_{\delta_{EL}L} \;\colon\; \overline{\phi}_a \;\mapsto\; \frac{\delta_{EL} L}{\delta \phi^a} \,.

Consider then the graded symmetric algebra

Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]) Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right)

which is generated over Ω Σ 0,0(E)\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E) from the evolutionary vector fields in degree -1, and let

δ BV:Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1])⟶Sym Ω Σ 0,0(E)(Γ Σ ev(V ΣE)[−1]) \delta_{BV} \;\colon\; Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right) \;\longrightarrow\; Sym_{\Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E)}\left( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma E)[-1] \right)

be the unique extension of the linear map ι δ ELL\iota_{\delta_{EL}L} to a derivation of degree +1 on this algebra.

The cochain cohomology of δ BV\delta_{BV} has the following interpretation:

in degree 0:

im(Γ Σ ev(V Σ(E))→δ BVΩ Σ 0,0(E)) im( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma(E)) \overset{\delta_{BV}}{\to} \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E))

is functions on the shell.

in degree 1:

im(Γ Σ ev(V Σ(E))→δ BVΩ Σ 0,0(E)) im( \Gamma_\Sigma^{ev}(V_\Sigma(E)) \overset{\delta_{BV}}{\to} \Omega^{0,0}_\Sigma(E))

is the gauge symmetries

Example

(energy-momentum? of the scalar field)

Consider the Lagrangian field theory of the free scalar field from (…)

\mathbf{L) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} \left( \eta^{\mu \nu}\phi_{,\mu} \phi_{,\nu} + m^2 \phi^2 \right) dvol_\Sigma \,.

For ν∈{0,1,⋯,p}\nu \in \{0, 1, \cdots, p\} consider the vector field

v ν≔ϕ ,ν∂ ϕ+ϕ ,μν∂ ϕ ,μ+⋯. v_\nu \;\coloneqq\; \phi_{,\nu} \partial_{\phi} + \phi_{,\mu \nu} \partial_{\phi_{,\mu}} + \cdots \,.

This describes infinitesimal translations of the fields in the direction of ∂ ν\partial_\nu.

And this is an infinitesimal symmetry, since

ι v νmathfdL=dLι ∂ νdvol Σ. \iota_{v_\nu} \mathf{d}\mathbf{L} = d L \iota_{\partial_\nu} dvol_\Sigma \,.

With the formula spring for the presymplectic potential

Θ=η μνϕ ,μδϕι ∂ νdvol Σ \Theta = \eta^{\mu \nu} \phi_{,\mu} \delta \phi \iota_{\partial_{\nu}} dvol_\Sigma

it follows that the corresponding consderved current is

T ν=Lι ∂ νdvol Σ−ι v νΘ=Lι ∂ νdvol Σ−η ρμϕ ,ρϕ ,νι ∂ μdvol Σ=(δ ν μL−η ρμϕ ,ρϕ ,ν⏟=:T ν μ)ι ∂ μdvol Σ. T_\nu \;=\; L \iota_{\partial_\nu} dvol_\Sigma - \iota_{v_\nu}\Theta = L \iota_{\partial_\nu} dvol_\Sigma - \eta^{\rho \mu} \phi_{,\rho} \phi_{,\nu} \iota_{\partial_\mu} dvol_\Sigma = ( \underset{=: T^\mu_\nu}{ \underbrace{ \delta^\mu_\nu L - \eta^{\rho \mu} \phi_{,\rho} \phi_{,\nu} } } ) \iota_{\partial_\mu} dvol_\Sigma \,.

This conserved current is called the energy-momentum tensor.
