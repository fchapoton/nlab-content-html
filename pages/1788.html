Definition

(star algebra)

A star ring is a ring RR equipped with

  • a linear map

    (−) *:R⟶R(-)^\ast \;\colon\; R \longrightarrow R

such that

  • (involution) ((−) *) *=id((-)^\ast)^\ast = id;

  • (antihomomorphism)

    1. (ab) *=b *a *(a b)^\ast = b^\ast a^\ast for all a,b∈Ra,b \in R

    2. 1 *=11^\ast = 1.

A homomorphism of star-rings

f:(R 1,(−) *)⟶(R 2,(−) †) f \;\colon\; (R_1, (-)^\ast) \longrightarrow (R_2, (-)^\dagger)

is a homomorphism of the underlying rings

f:R 1⟶R 2 f \;\colon\; R_1 \longrightarrow R_2

which respects the star-involutions in that

f∘(−) *=(−) †∘f. f \circ (-)^\ast \;=\; (-)^\dagger \circ f \,.

A star algebra 𝒜\mathcal{A} over a commutative star-ring RR in an associative algebra 𝒜\mathcal{A} over RR such that the inclusion

R↪𝒜 R \hookrightarrow \mathcal{A}

is a star-homomorphism.

Examples

(complex number-valued observables are star-algebra under pointwise product and pointwise complex conjugation)

The complex numbers ℂ\mathbb{C} carry the structure of a star-ring (def. 1) with star-operation given by complex conjugation.

Given any space XX, then the algebra of functions on XX with values in the complex numbers carries the structure of a star-algebra over the star-ring ℂ\mathbb{C} (def. 1) with star-operation given by pointwise complex conjugation in the complex numbers.

In particular for (E,L)(E,\mathbf{L}) a Lagrangian field theory (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) then its on-shell observables Obs(E,L)Obs(E,\mathbf{L}) (def. \ref{Observable}) carry the structure of a star-algebra this way.

Definition

(state on a star-algebra)

Given a star algebra (𝒜,(−) *)(\mathcal{A}, (-)^\ast) (def. 1) over the star-ring of complex numbers (def. 1) a state is a function to the complex numbers

⟨−⟩:Obs Σ⟶ℂ \langle -\rangle \;\colon\; Obs_\Sigma \longrightarrow \mathbb{C}

such that

  1. (linearity) this is a complex-linear map:

    ⟨c 1A 1+c 2A 2⟩=c 1⟨A 1⟩+c 2⟨A 2⟩ \left\langle c_1 A_1 + c_2 A_2 \right\rangle \;=\; c_1 \langle A_1 \rangle + c_2 \langle A_2 \rangle

  2. (positivity) for all A∈ObsA \in Obs we have that

    ⟨A *A⟩≥0∈ℝ \langle A^\ast A \rangle \geq 0 \;\in\; \mathbb{R}

    where on the left A *A^\ast is the star-operation from

  3. (normalization)

    ⟨1⟩=1. \langle 1 \rangle \;=\; 1 \,.

(e.g. Bordemann-Waldmann 96, Fredenhagen-Rejzner 12, def. 2.4, Khavkine-Moretti 15, def. 6)

Remark

(probability theoretic interpretation of state on a star-algebra)

A star algebra 𝒜\mathcal{A} (def. 1) equipped with a state 𝒜⟶⟨−⟩ℂ\mathcal{A} \overset{\langle -\rangle}{\longrightarrow} \mathbb{C} (def. \ref{States}) is also called a quantum probability space, at least when 𝒜\mathcal{A} is in fact a von Neumann algebra.

For this interpretation we think of each element A∈𝒜A \in \mathcal{A} as an observable as in example 1 and of the state as assigning expectation values.

Remark

(states form a convex set)

For 𝒜\mathcal{A} a unital star-algebra (def. 1), the set of states on 𝒜\mathcal{A} according to def. 2 is naturally a convex set: For ⟨(−)⟩ 1,⟨−⟩ 2:𝒜→ℂ\langle (-)\rangle_1, \langle - \rangle_2 \colon \mathcal{A} \to \mathbb{C} two states then for every p∈[0,1]⊂ℝp \in [0,1] \subset \mathbb{R} also the linear combination

𝒜 ⟶p⟨(−)⟩ 1+(1−p)⟨(−)⟩ 2 ℂ A ↦ p⟨A⟩ 1+(1−p)⟨A⟩ 2 \array{ \mathcal{A} &\overset{p \langle (-)\rangle_1 + (1-p) \langle (-)\rangle_2}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{C} \\ A &\mapsto& p \langle A \rangle_1 + (1-p) \langle A \rangle_2 }

is a state.

Definition

(pure state)

A state ρ:𝒜→ℂ\rho \colon \mathcal{A} \to \mathbb{C} on a unital star-algebra (def. 2) is called a pure state if it is extremal in the convex set of all states (remark 2) in that an identification

⟨(−)⟩=p⟨(−)⟩ 1+(1−p)⟨(−)⟩ 2 \langle (- )\rangle = p \langle (-)\rangle_1 + (1-p) \langle (-)\rangle_2

for p∈(0,1)p \in (0,1) implies that ⟨(−)⟩ 1=⟨(−)⟩ 2\langle (-) \rangle_1 = \langle (-)\rangle_2 (hence =⟨(−)⟩= \langle (-)\rangle).

Proposition

(classical probability measure as state on measurable functions)

For Ω\Omega classical probability space, hence a measure space which normalized total measure ∫ Ωdμ=1\int_\Omega d\mu = 1, let 𝒜cloneqqL 1(Ω)\mathcal{A} \cloneqq L^1(\Omega) be the algebra of Lebesgue measurable functions with values in the complex numbers, regarded as a star algebra (def. 1) by pointwise complex conjugation as in example 1. Then forming the expectation value with respect to μ\mu defines a state (def. 2):

L 1(Ω) ⟶⟨(−)⟩ μ ℂ A ↦ ∫ ΩAdμ \array{ L^1(\Omega) &\overset{\langle (-)\rangle_\mu}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{C} \\ A &\mapsto& \int_\Omega A d\mu }
Example

(elements of a Hilbert space as pure states on bounded operators)

Let ℋ\mathcal{H} be a complex separable Hilbert space with inner product ⟨−,−⟩\langle -,-\rangle and let 𝒜≔ℬ(ℋ)\mathcal{A} \coloneqq \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}) be the algebra of bounded operators, regarded as a star algebra (def. 1) under forming adjoint operators. Then for every element ψ∈ℋ\psi \in \mathcal{H} of unit norm ⟨ψ,ψ⟩=1\langle \psi,\psi\rangle = 1 there is the state (def. 2) given by

ℬ(ℋ) ⟶⟨(−)⟩ ψ ℂ A ↦ ⟨ψ|A|ψ⟩ ≔ ⟨ψ,Aψ⟩ \array{ \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}) &\overset{\langle (-)\rangle_\psi}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{C} \\ A &\mapsto& \langle \psi \vert\, A \, \vert \psi \rangle &\coloneqq& \langle \psi, A \psi \rangle }

These are pure states (def. 3).

More general states in this case are given by density matrices.

Theorem

(GNS construction)

Given

  1. a star-algebra, 𝒜\mathcal{A} (def. 1);

  2. a state?, ⟨(−)⟩:𝒜→ℂ\langle (-)\rangle \;\colon\; \mathcal{A} \to \mathbb{C} (def. 2)

there exists

  1. a star-representation

    π:𝒜⟶End(ℋ) \pi \;\colon\; \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow End(\mathcal{H})

    of 𝒜\mathcal{A} on some Hilbert space ℋ\mathcal{H}

  2. a cyclic vector ψ∈ℋ\psi \in \mathcal{H}

such that ⟨(−)⟩\langle (-)\rangle is the state corresponding to ψ\psi via example 1, in that

⟨A⟩ =⟨ψ|A|ψ⟩ ≔⟨ψ,π(A)ψ⟩ \begin{aligned} \langle A \rangle & = \langle \psi \vert\, A \, \vert \psi \rangle \\ & \coloneqq \langle \psi , \pi(A) \psi \rangle \end{aligned}

for all A∈𝒜A \in \mathcal{A}.

(Khavkine-Moretti 15, theorem 1)

Definition

(classical state)

Given a Lagrangian field theory (E,L)(E,\mathbf{L}) (def. \ref{LocalLagrangianDensityOnSecondOrderJetBundleOfTrivialVectorBundleOverMinkowskiSpacetime}) then a classical state is a state on the star algebra (def. 2) of on-shell observables (example 1):

⟨−⟩:Obs(E,L)⟶ℂ. \langle -\rangle \;\colon\; Obs(E,\mathbf{L}) \longrightarrow \mathbb{C} \,.
