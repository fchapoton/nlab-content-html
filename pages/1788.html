Example

(Feynman perturbation series)

Let (E BV-BRST,L′,Δ H)(E_{\text{BV-BRST}}, \mathbf{L}', \Delta_H ) be a relativistic free vacuum according to def. \ref{VacuumFree}, and let

gS int+jA∈LocObs(E BV-BRST)[[ℏ,g,h]] g S_{int} + j A \;\in\; LocObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})[ [ \hbar, g, h ] ]

be a local observable.

By prop. \ref{FeynmanPerturbationSeriesAwayFromCoincidingPoints} every choice of perturbative S-matrix (def. \ref{LagrangianFieldTheoryPerturbativeScattering})

𝒮(gS int+jA)∈PolyObs(E BV-BRST) mc((ℏ))[[g,j]]+ \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) \;\in\; PolyObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})_{mc}((\hbar))[ [ g, j ] ] +

has an expansion as a formal power series of the form

𝒮(gS int+jA)=∑Γ∈𝒢Γ norm(gS int+jA), \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) \;=\; \underset{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}}{\sum} \Gamma_{norm}(g S_{int} + j A) \,,

where the series is over all Feynman diagrams Γ\Gamma all whose vertices are labeled by gS int+jAg S_{int} + jA (def. \ref{FeynmanDiagram}), and the summands are the corresponding ("re"-)normalized (def. \ref{ExtensionOfTimeOrderedProoductsRenormalization}) Feynman amplitudes (prop. \ref{FeynmanPerturbationSeriesAwayFromCoincidingPoints}).

Expressed in this form the S-matrix is known as the Feynman perturbation series.

Definition

(effective action)

Let (E BV-BRST,L′,Δ H)(E_{\text{BV-BRST}}, \mathbf{L}', \Delta_H ) be a relativistic free vacuum according to def. \ref{VacuumFree}, let 𝒮\mathcal{S} be an S-matrix scheme for perturbative QFT around this vacuum (def. \ref{LagrangianFieldTheoryPerturbativeScattering}) and let

gS int+jA∈LocObs(E BV-BRST)[[ℏ,g,h]] g S_{int} + j A \;\in\; LocObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})[ [ \hbar, g, h ] ]

be a local observable.

Recall that for each Feynman diagram Γ\Gamma with all vertices labeled by gS int+jAg S_{int} + j A (def. \ref{FeynmanDiagram}) the Feynman perturbation series for 𝒮(gS int+jA)\mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) (example 1)

𝒮(gS int+jA)=∑Γ∈𝒢Γ(gS int+jA) \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) \;=\; \underset{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}}{\sum} \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A)

contributes with a ("re"-)nromalized Feynman amplitude Γ(gS int+jA)∈PolyObs(E BV-BRST)((ℏ))[[g,j]]\Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \in PolyObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})((\hbar))[ [ g, j ] ].

We say that the corresponding effective action is iℏi \hbar times the vacuum expectation value of the sub-series

S eff≔iℏ∑Γ∈𝒢 connΓ(gS int+jA)∈PolyObs(E BV-BRST)((ℏ))[[g,j]] S_{eff} \;\coloneqq\; i \hbar \underset{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{conn}}{\sum} \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \;\in\; PolyObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})((\hbar))[ [ g, j ] ]

of Feynman amplitudes that are labeled only by the connected Feynman diagrams Γ∈𝒢 conn⊂𝒢\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}_{conn} \subset \mathcal{G}.

The terminology “effective action” is due to the following fact:

Proposition

(effective action is logarithm of S-matrix)

Let (E BV-BRST,L′,Δ H)(E_{\text{BV-BRST}}, \mathbf{L}', \Delta_H ) be a relativistic free vacuum according to def. \ref{VacuumFree}, let 𝒮\mathcal{S} be an S-matrix scheme for perturbative QFT around this vacuum (def. \ref{LagrangianFieldTheoryPerturbativeScattering}) and let

gS int+jA∈LocObs(E BV-BRST)[[ℏ,g,h]] g S_{int} + j A \;\in\; LocObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})[ [ \hbar, g, h ] ]

be a local observable and let

S eff∈PolyObs(E BV-BRST)((ℏ))[[g,j]] S_{eff} \;\in\; PolyObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})((\hbar))[ [ g, j] ]

be the corresponding effective action (def. 1).

Then then S-matrix for gS int+jAg S_{int} + j A is the exponential of the effective action with respect to the pointwise product (−)⋅(−)(-)\cdot (-) of observables (this def.):

𝒮(gS int+jA) =exp ⋅(1iℏS eff) ≔1+1iℏS eff+1(iℏ) 2S eff⋅S eff+1(iℏ) 3S eff⋅S eff⋅S eff+⋯ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) & = \exp_\cdot\left( \tfrac{1}{i \hbar} S_{eff} \right) \\ & \coloneqq 1 + \frac{1}{i \hbar} S_{eff} + \frac{1}{(i \hbar)^2} S_{eff} \cdot S_{eff} + \frac{1}{(i \hbar)^3} S_{eff} \cdot S_{eff} \cdot S_{eff} + \cdots \end{aligned}

Moreover, this relation passes to the vacuum expectation values:

⟨𝒮(gS int+jA)⟩=e 1iℏ⟨S eff⟩. \left\langle \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) \right\rangle \;=\; e^{\tfrac{1}{i \hbar} \langle S_{eff}\rangle} \,.

Conversely the vacuum expectation value of the effective action is to the logarithm of that of the S-matrix:

⟨S eff⟩=iℏln⟨𝒮(gS int+jA)⟩. \left\langle S_{eff} \right\rangle \;=\; i \hbar \, \ln \left\langle \mathcal{S}(g S_{int} + j A) \right\rangle \,.
Proof

By prop. \ref{TimeOrderedProductAwayFromDiagonal} the contributions to the S-matrix away from coinciding interaction points are given by the star product iinduced by the Feynman propagator, and specifically, by prop. \ref{FeynmanPerturbationSeriesAwayFromCoincidingPoints}, the Feynman amplitudes are given this way. Now by definition of the star product (this def.) it is given by first contracting with powers of the Feynman propagator and then multiplying all resulting terms with the pointwise product of observables. From this and the nature of the combinatorial factor in the definition of the Feynman amplitudes (prop. \ref{FeynmanPerturbationSeriesAwayFromCoincidingPoints}) it is clear that for

Γ=Γ 1⊔Γ 2⊔⋯⊔Γ n∈𝒢 \Gamma = \Gamma_1 \sqcup \Gamma_2 \sqcup \cdots \sqcup \Gamma_n \;\in\; \mathcal{G}

a disjoint union of Feynman diagrams, then then corresponding Feynman amplitudes multiply by the pointwise product

Γ(gS int+jA)=Γ 1(gS int+jA)⋅Γ 2(gS int+jA)⋅⋯⋅Γ n(gS int+jA). \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \;=\; \Gamma_1(g S_{int} + j A) \cdot \Gamma_2(g S_{int} + j A) \cdot \cdots \cdot \Gamma_n(g S_{int} + j A) \,.

This implies that the summands in the nnth powers of 1iℏ\frac{1}{i \hbar} times the effective action are precisely the Feynman amplitudes of Feynman diagrams with nn connected components, where each such appears with multiplicity given by the factorial of nn:

1n!(1iℏS eff) n=Γ(gS int+jA)∑Γ=⊔j=1nΓ jΓ j∈𝒢 conn. \frac{1}{n!} \left( \frac{1}{i \hbar} S_{eff} \right)^n \;=\; \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \underset{ { \Gamma = \underoverset{j = 1}{n}{\sqcup} \Gamma_j } \atop { \Gamma_j \in \mathcal{G}_{conn} } }{\sum} \,.

It follows that

exp ⋅(1iℏS int) =∑n∈ℕ∑Γ=⊔j=1nΓ jΓ j∈𝒢 connΓ(gS int+jA) =∑Γ∈𝒢Γ(gS int+jA) \begin{aligned} \exp_\cdot\left( \frac{1}{i \hbar} S_{int} \right) & = \underset{n \in \mathbb{N}}{\sum} \underset{ { \Gamma = \underoverset{j = 1}{n}{\sqcup} \Gamma_j } \atop { \Gamma_j \in \mathcal{G}_{conn} } }{\sum} \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \\ & = \underset{\Gamma \in \mathcal{G}}{\sum} \Gamma(g S_{int} + j A) \end{aligned}

yields the Feynman perturbation series by expressing it as a series organized by number of connected components.

To conclude the proof it is now sufficient to observe that taking vacuum expectation values of polynomial observables respects the pointwise product of observables

⟨A 1⋅ 2⟩=⟨A 1⟩⟨A 2⟩ \left\langle A_1 \cdot _2\right\rangle \;=\; \left\langle A_1 \right\rangle \, \left\langle A_2 \right\rangle

(since the Hadamard vacuum state ⟨−⟩:PolyObs(E BV-BRST)[[ℏ,g,j]]→ℂ[[ℏ,g,j]]\langle -\rangle \colon PolyObs(E_{\text{BV-BRST}})[ [ \hbar, g, j] ] \to \mathbb{C}[ [\hbar, g, j ] ] simply picks the zero-order monomial term, by this prop.).

Proposition

(loop order of Feynman diagrams)

Let Γ\Gamma be a Feynman diagram (def. \ref{FeynmanDiagram}) whose underlying graph is planar. Then the order of ℏ\hbar at which its Feynman amplitude (def. \ref{FeynmanPerturbationSeriesAwayFromCoincidingPoints}) contributes in the S-matrix (def. \ref{LagrangianFieldTheoryPerturbativeScattering}) is

ℏ L(Γ)−1, \hbar^{L(\Gamma) - 1} \,,

where L(Γ)∈ℕL(\Gamma) \in \mathbb{N} is the number of faces of the planar graph Γ\Gamma, here called the loop number of the diagram.

Accordingly, the order in ℏ\hbar at which the Feynman amplitude of a (planar) Feynman diagram contributes is often referred to as the loop order.

The contributions at loop order L(Γ)=0L(\Gamma) = 0 are often called the tree level contributions.
