Idea

The generalization of an over-category to enriched categories.

Definition

Let (V,⊗,I)(V,\otimes,I) be a monoidal category with pullbacks, CC a VV-enriched category, and x∈Cx\in C an object. The enriched over-category (or enriched slice category) C/xC/x is the following VV-category:

  • its objects are the morphisms f:y→xf:y\to x in (the underlying ordinary category? of) CC, i.e. morphisms I→C(y,x)I\to C(y,x) in VV.

  • its hom-object from f:y→xf:y\to x to g:z→xg:z\to x is the pullback in VV:

The dual concept is the enriched under-category or enriched co-slice category.

Properties

  • C/xC/x can be described as the comma object of Id:C→CId:C\to C and x:I→Cx : I \to C in the 2-category VCatV Cat, where II denotes the unit enriched category.

  • All hom-objects of C/xC/x are augmented, i.e. come with a map to the unit object. This seems somewhat curious unless VV is cartesian monoidal (or at least semicartesian) in which case II is the terminal object 11.

  • Any morphism f:x→yf:x\to y in CC induces a VV-functor f !:C/x→C/yf_!:C/x \to C/y. If CC has VV-enriched pullbacks, then f !f_! has a right VV-adjoint f *f^*. If CC is a locally cartesian closed enriched category?, then f *f^* has a further right VV-adjoint f *f_*.
