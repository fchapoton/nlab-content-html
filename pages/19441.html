Idea

The inductive tensor product E⊗ iFE\otimes_i F of a pair of topological vector spaces EE, and FF is the target of the universal separately continuous bilinear map E×F→E⊗ iFE\times F \to E\otimes_i F.

Definition

Given a pair of locally convex topological vector spaces (lctvs) EE and FF, the inductive tensor product is the locally convex tvs E⊗ iFE\otimes_i F with underlying vector space the algebraic tensor product, and equipped with the finest locally convex linear topology such that E×F→E⊗ iFE\times F \to E\otimes_i F is separately continuous, where E×FE\times F has the product topology.

Compare this to the (pre-completed) projective tensor product ⊗ p\otimes_p, which is equipped with the finest locally convex linear topology such that the canonical map from E×FE\times F is jointly continuous.

Sometimes one will wish to consider this tensor product in the category of complete lctvs, and so the result is the topological completion E⊗¯FE\overline{\otimes} F of E⊗ iFE\otimes_i F (eg by adding in the limits of Cauchy nets), which is the analogue of the (complete) projective tensor product ⊗^\widehat{\otimes}.

Properties

Proposition

Let EE and FF be metrizable tvs, and T:E×F→GT\colon E\times F \to G a separately continuous bilinear function. If one of the following conditions is true

  1. EE is a Baire space (for example, completely metrizable)
  2. EE is a barreled topological vector space (eg is a Frechet space) and GG is locally convex.

then TT is jointly continuous.

For a proof see (Schaefer-Wolff 1999, corollary to Theorem 5.1)

Notice that there are canonical comparison maps E⊗ iF→E⊗ pFE\otimes_i F \to E\otimes_p F and E⊗¯F→E⊗^FE\overline{\otimes} F \to E \widehat{\otimes} F.

Theorem

Let EE and FF be metrizable lctvs, and assume one of the conditions in Proposition 1 holds. Then the comparison maps from the inductive tensor products to the projective tensor products are isomorphisms.

Corollary

If EE and FF are Fréchet spaces, then E⊗¯F→∼E⊗^FE\overline{\otimes} F \stackrel{\sim}{\to} E \widehat{\otimes} F.

Hence for Banach spaces and Hilbert spaces the inductive tensor product also agrees with the projective tensor product. It is for this reason that one can define locally convex algebra?s with the inductive tensor product, as this reduces the the usual jointly-continuous multiplication for Banach algebras, C *C^*-algebras etc.
