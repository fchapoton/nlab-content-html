Idea

The (right) derived functor of an enriched hom-functor.

Definition

For enriched model categories

We discuss the definition of the derived hom-functor (Def. 2 below) for enriched model categories (recalled as Def. 1 below). The key point is to observe (Lemma 1 below) that the axioms on enriched model categories imply that the ordinary enriched hom-functor is a homotopical functor when its first argument is cofibrant and its second argument is fibrant, so that its right derived functor exists.

\,

Definition

(enriched model category)

Let 𝒱\mathcal{V} be a monoidal model category. Then a 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched model category is a category 𝒞\mathcal{C} equipped with

  1. the structure of 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched category, which is also tensored and cotensored over 𝒱\mathcal{V};

  2. the structure of a model category,

such that these two structures are compatible in the following way:

  • for every cofibration X→fYX \overset{f}{\to} Y and every fibration A→gBA \overset{g}{\to} B in 𝒞\mathcal{C}, the induced pullback powering-morphism of hom-objects in 𝒱\mathcal{V}

    (1)𝒞(Y,A)⟶𝒞(X,A)×𝒞(X,B)𝒞(Y,B) \mathcal{C}(Y,A) \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}(X,A) \underset{\mathcal{C}(X,B)}{\times} \mathcal{C}(Y,B)

    is a fibration, and is a weak equivalence as soon as one of the two morphisms is a weak equivalence in 𝒞\mathcal{C}.

Lemma

(in enriched model category the enriched hom-functor out of cofibrant into fibrant is homotopical functor)

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be an enriched model category (Def. 1).

If Y∈𝒞Y \in \mathcal{C} is a cofibrant object, then the enriched hom-functor out of XX

𝒞(Y,−):𝒞⟶𝒱 \mathcal{C}(Y,-) \;\colon\; \mathcal{C} \longrightarrow \mathcal{V}

preserves fibrations and acyclic fibrations.

If A∈𝒞A \in \mathcal{C} is a fibrant object, then the enriched hom-functor into XX

𝒞(−,A):𝒞 op⟶𝒱 \mathcal{C}(-,A) \;\colon\; \mathcal{C}^{op} \longrightarrow \mathcal{V}

sends cofibrations and acyclic cofibrations in 𝒞\mathcal{C} to fibrations and acyclic fibrations, respectively, in 𝒱\mathcal{V}.

Proof

In the first case, consider the comparison morphism (1) for X=∅X =\emptyset the initial object, in the second case consider it for B=*B = \ast the terminal object.

Since 𝒞\mathcal{C} is tensored and cotensored over 𝒱\mathcal{V}, it follows (by this Prop that

𝒞(∅,−)≃*AAandAA𝒞(−,*)*∈𝒱. \mathcal{C}(\emptyset, -) \;\simeq\; \ast \phantom{AA} \text{and} \phantom{AA} \mathcal{C}(-,\ast) \;\ast\; \;\;\; \in \mathcal{V} \,.

This means that in the first case the comparison morphism

𝒞(Y,A)⟶𝒞(X,A)×𝒞(X,B)𝒞(Y,B) \mathcal{C}(Y,A) \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}(X,A) \underset{\mathcal{C}(X,B)}{\times} \mathcal{C}(Y,B)

(1) becomes equal to the top morphism in the following diagram

𝒞(Y,A) ⟶𝒞(Y,g) 𝒞(Y,B) ↓ ↓ * ⟶AAA * \array{ \mathcal{C}(Y,A) &\overset{\mathcal{C}(Y,g)}{\longrightarrow}& \mathcal{C}(Y,B) \\ \Big\downarrow && \Big\downarrow \\ \ast &\underset{\phantom{AAA}}{\longrightarrow}& \ast }

while in the second case it becomes equal to the left morphism in

𝒞(Y,A) ⟶𝒞(Y,g) * 𝒞(f,A)↓ ↓ 𝒞(X,A) ⟶AAA * \array{ \mathcal{C}(Y,A) &\overset{\phantom{\mathcal{C}(Y,g)}}{\longrightarrow}& \ast \\ {}^{\mathllap{ \mathcal{C}(f,A) }}\Big\downarrow && \Big\downarrow \\ \mathcal{C}(X,A) &\underset{\phantom{AAA}}{\longrightarrow}& \ast }

Hence the claim follows by the defining condition on the comparison morphism (1) in an enriched model category.

Definition

(derived hom-functor of an enriched model category)

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be an enriched model category (Def. 1).

By Lemma 1 and by Ken Brown's lemma, the enriched hom-functor has a right derived functor (this Def.) when its first argument is cofibrant and its second argument is fibrant. This is called the derived hom-functor

ℝhom:Ho(𝒞) op×Ho(𝒞)⟶Ho(𝒱) \mathbb{R}hom \;\colon\; Ho(\mathcal{C})^{op} \times Ho(\mathcal{C}) \longrightarrow Ho(\mathcal{V})

In the presence of functorial cofibrant resolution QQ and fibrant resolution PP this is given by the ordinary enriched hom-functor 𝒞(−,−)\mathcal{C}(-,-) as

ℝhom(X,Y)≃𝒞(QX,PY). \mathbb{R}hom(X,Y) \;\simeq\; \mathcal{C}(Q X, P Y) \,.
