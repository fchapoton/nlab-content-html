Idea

Just like a right AA-module can be tensored (over AA) with a left AA-module to obtain an abelian group, a functor T:𝒞 op→𝒱T : \mathcal{C}^\op \to \mathcal{V} can be tensored (over 𝒞\mathcal{C}) with a functor S:𝒞→𝒱S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{V} to obtain an object of 𝒱\mathcal{V}. Here, 𝒱\mathcal{V} can be an arbitrary monoidal category. A simple case is 𝒱=Set\mathcal{V} = Set with the cartesian product as monoidal structure.

Definition

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a category. Let (𝒱,⊗)(\mathcal{V},{\otimes}) be a monoidal category. Let T:𝒞 op→𝒱T : \mathcal{C}^\op \to \mathcal{V} and S:𝒞→𝒱S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{V} be functors. Then their tensor product T⊗ 𝒞ST \otimes_{\mathcal{C}} S is defined (if it exists) as the coend

T⊗ 𝒞S≔∫ cT(c)⊗S(c). T \otimes_{\mathcal{C}} S \coloneqq \int^c T(c) \otimes S(c).

The following slight variation is also important. Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a category. Let 𝒟\mathcal{D} be a category with all coproducts, so that the copower X⋅d=∐ x∈Xd∈𝒟X \cdot d = \coprod_{x \in X} d \in \mathcal{D} exists for any set XX and any object d∈𝒟d \in \mathcal{D}. Let T:𝒞 op→SetT : \mathcal{C}^\op \to Set and S:𝒞→𝒟S : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D} be functors. Then their tensor product is (if it exists) the coend

T⊗ 𝒞S≔∫ cT(c)⋅S(c). T \otimes_{\mathcal{C}} S \coloneqq \int^c T(c) \cdot S(c).

Intuition for the tensor product of functors can be gained by relating it to the tensor product of modules (see the first example below) and by a picture involving gluing specifications (see below).

Examples

  • Recall that a ring AA can be considered as an Ab-enriched category and that a right and a left module gives rise to an additive functor A op→AbA^\op \to Ab respectively A→AbA \to Ab. Then their tensor product as functors, calculated in the Ab-enriched setting and using the ordinary tensor product of abelian groups as monoidal structure on AbAb, coincides with their usual tensor product.

  • Let XX be a simplicial set and st:Δ→Topst : \Delta \to Top be the functor which associates to [n][n] the topological standard nn-simplex. Then the geometric realization of XX can be expressed as the tensor product |X|=X⊗ Δst|X| = X \otimes_\Delta st.

  • Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a small category and let F:𝒞→𝒟F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D} be a functor into a cocomplete category 𝒟\mathcal{D}. Since the category PSh(𝒞)PSh(\mathcal{C}) of presheaves on 𝒞\mathcal{C} is the free cocompletion of 𝒞\mathcal{C}, the functor FF induces a functor F^:PSh(𝒞)→𝒟\hat F : PSh(\mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{D}. This functor can be explicitly described as F^(X)=X⊗ 𝒞F\hat F(X) = X \otimes_\mathcal{C} F.

  • Let Y:𝒞→PSh(𝒞)Y : \mathcal{C} \to PSh(\mathcal{C}) denote the Yoneda embedding. Let F:𝒞 op→SetF : \mathcal{C}^\op \to Set be a presheaf on 𝒞\mathcal{C}. Then F⊗Y=FF \otimes Y = F. This fact is the co-Yoneda lemma (also referred to as the ninja Yoneda lemma in some circles).

  • In some sense, representable functors generalize free modules: Recall A n⊗ AM≅M nA^n \otimes_A M \cong M^n. Similarly,

    Hom 𝒞(⋅,c)⊗ 𝒞S=S(c). \Hom_\mathcal{C}(\cdot, c) \otimes_\mathcal{C} S = S(c).

    This follows from some coend manipulations:

    Hom(Hom 𝒞(⋅,c)⊗ 𝒞S,t) = ∫ c′∈𝒞Hom(Hom 𝒞(c′,c)×S(c′),t) = ∫ c′∈𝒞Hom(Hom 𝒞(c′,c),Hom 𝒟(S(c′),t)) = Nat(Hom 𝒞(⋅,c),Hom 𝒟(S(⋅),t)) = Hom 𝒟(S(c),t). \begin{array}{rcl} \Hom(\Hom_\mathcal{C}(\cdot, c) \otimes_\mathcal{C} S, t) &=& \int_{c' \in \mathcal{C}} \Hom(\Hom_\mathcal{C}(c',c) \times S(c'), t) \\ &=& \int_{c' \in \mathcal{C}} \Hom(\Hom_\mathcal{C}(c',c), \Hom_{\mathcal{D}}(S(c'), t)) \\ &=& Nat(\Hom_\mathcal{C}(\cdot, c), \Hom_\mathcal{D}(S(\cdot), t)) \\ &=& \Hom_\mathcal{D}(S(c),t). \end{array}

    From this perspective, the representable functor Hom 𝒞(⋅,c)Hom_\mathcal{C}(\cdot, c) looks like a delta distribution concentrated at cc.

Intuition using gluing specifications

Recall that a presheaf F:𝒞 op→SetF : \mathcal{C}^\mathrm{op} \to \mathrm{Set} can be seen as “gluing specification”: If G:𝒞→𝒟G : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D} is some functor into a cocomplete category 𝒟\mathcal{D}, this gluing specification can be realized as colim s∈F(X)G(X)\operatorname{colim}_{s \in F(X)} G(X). This colimit can also be written as the coend

∫ X∈𝒞F(X)⋅G(X), \int^{X \in \mathcal{C}} F(X) \cdot G(X),

that is as the tensor product F⊗ 𝒞GF \otimes_\mathcal{C} G. The tensor product can therefore be pictured as the G(X)G(X)‘s, glued as specified by FF.

  • Since the Yoneda embedding YY includes an object into the category of formal gluing specifications, gluing the Y(X)Y(X)‘s as specified by FF simply yields in FF; thus F⊗ 𝒞Y=FF \otimes_\mathcal{C} Y = F.

  • Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be specifically the simplex category Δ\Delta. Then FF is just a simplicial set, so the intuition of FF as a gluing specification is even more vivid. Tensoring with st:Δ→Topst : \Delta \to Top realizes this specification in the category of topological spaces, using standard nn-simplices as building blocks, and therefore yields the geometric realization |F||F|.
