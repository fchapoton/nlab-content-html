Idea

The ordinary Euclidean group of ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n is the group generated from the rigid translation action of ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n on itself and rotations about the origin.

The super Euclidean group is analogously the supergroup of translations and rotations of the supermanifold ℝ p|q\mathbb{R}^{p|q}.

Its super Lie algebra should be the super Poincare Lie algebra (up to the signature of the metric).

The following description of the super Euclidean group (once it is finished, and polished) is due to Stephan Stolz and Peter Teichner.

The data needed to define the super Euclidean group is

  1. VV a dd-dimensional inner product space

  2. a spinor representation Δ *\Delta^* of Spin(V)Spin(V)

  3. a Spin(V)Spin(V)-equivariant map

    Γ:Δ *⊗ ℂΔ *→V⊗ ℝℂ \Gamma : \Delta^* \otimes_{\mathbb{C}} \Delta^* \to V \otimes_{\mathbb{R}} \mathbb{C}

where Spin(V)Spin(V) is the Spin group (see Clifford algebra for the moment).

Here is the construction of Eucl(ℝ d|δ)Eucl(\mathbb{R}^{d|\delta}) for

  • d=dim ℝVd = dim_{\mathbb{R}} V

remark δ\delta is a multiple of 2 [d−12]2^{[\frac{d-1}{2}]}

set

X=Π(V×Δ * ↓ V)=V×ΠΔ * X = \Pi \left( \array{ V \times \Delta^* \\ \downarrow \\ V} \right) = V \times \Pi \Delta^*

is a complex supermanifold of dimension (d|δ)(d|\delta)
