Idea

Roughly speaking, a C *C^*-correspondence between two C *C^*-algebras AA and BB is a generalised C *C^*-homomorphism from AA to BB.

Definition

Definition

Let AA and BB be C *C^*-algebras. An A,BA,B-correspondence is a pair (E,φ)(E,\varphi) consisting of a right Hilbert BB-module EE and a non-degerate C *C^*-homomorphism φ:A→ℒ B(E)\varphi \colon A\to \mathcal{L}_B(E); i.e., the image φ(A)E\varphi(A)E is dense in EE (as Hilbert BB-modules).

We write

A→(E,φ)B, A\stackrel{(E,\varphi)}{\to} B,

or just A→EBA\stackrel{E}{\to}B, and call this a C *C^*-correspondence.

Properties

Composition of correspondences

Definition

Let A⟶(E,φ)BA\stackrel{(E,\varphi)}{\longrightarrow}B and B⟶(F,ψ)CB\stackrel{(F,\psi)}{\longrightarrow}C be C *C^*-correspondences. Then the internal tensor product E⊗ ψFE\otimes_{\psi}F is a Hilbert right CC-module. The composition

A⟶(E,φ)∘(F,ψ)C A \stackrel{(E,\varphi)\circ(F,\psi)}{\longrightarrow} C

is defined by the the pair (E⊗ ψF,φ⊗ψ)(E\otimes_{\psi}F,\varphi\otimes \psi), where

φ⊗ψ: A → ℒ C(E⊗ ψF) a ↦ φ(a)(⋅)⊗ ψ(⋅) \begin{array}{lccc} \varphi\otimes\psi \colon & A & \to & \mathcal{L}_C(E\otimes_{\psi}F) \\ & a & \mapsto & \varphi(a)(\cdot)\otimes_{\psi}(\cdot) \end{array}

Unitary intertwiners

Definition

Let A⟶(E,φ)BA\stackrel{(E,\varphi)}{\longrightarrow}B and A⟶(F,ψ)BA\stackrel{(F,\psi)}{\longrightarrow}B be C *C^*-correspondences. A unitary intertwiner

u:E⇒F u\colon E\Rightarrow F

is a unitary u∈ℒ B(E,F)u\in \mathcal{L}_B(E,F) such that for all a∈Aa\in A the following diagram commutes

E ⟶φ(a) E u↓ ↓ u F ⟶ψ(a) F \array{E & \stackrel{\varphi(a)}{\longrightarrow} & E\\ ^u\downarrow && \downarrow^u\\ F & \stackrel{\psi(a)}{\longrightarrow} & F }
