          Definition

          Definition

          (causal order)

          Let Σ\Sigma be a spacetime (a Lorentzian manifold equipped with time orientation).

          Consider the relation on the set P(Σ)P(\Sigma) of subsets of spacetime which says a subset S 1⊂ΣS_1 \subset \Sigma is not prior to a subset S 2⊂ΣS_2 \subset \Sigma, denoted S 1∨∧S 2S_1 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_2, if S 1S_1 does not intersect the causal past of S 2S_2, or equivalently that S 2S_2 does not intersect the causal future of S 1S_1:

          S 1∨∧S 2 ≔S 1∩V¯ −(S 2)=∅ ⇔S 2∩V¯ +(S 1)=∅. \begin{aligned} S_1 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_2 & \;\coloneqq\; S_1 \cap \overline{V}^-(S_2) = \emptyset \\ & \Leftrightarrow S_2 \cap \overline{V}^+(S_1) = \emptyset \end{aligned} \,.

          If S 1∨∧S 2S_1 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_2 and S 2∨∧S 1S_2 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_1 we say that the two subsets are spacelike separated and write

          S 1><S 2≔S 1∨∧S 2andS 2∨∧S 1. S_1 {\gt\!\!\!\!\lt} S_2 \;\;\;\coloneqq\;\;\; S_1 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_2 \;\text{and}\; S_2 {\vee\!\!\!\wedge} S_1 \,.

          (e.g. Epstein-Glaser 73, p. 5 (273))
