Idea

In stable homotopy theory the rational Bousfield localization of spectra, hence ℚ\mathbb{Q}-localization L SℚL_{S\mathbb{Q}}, is accompanied dually by “ℚ\mathbb{Q}-acyclification” G SℚG_{S\mathbb{Q}}, forming a natural homotopy fiber sequence

G SℚX⟶X⟶L SℚX G_{S \mathbb{Q}} X \longrightarrow X \longrightarrow L_{S\mathbb{Q}} X

(by this proposition).

This G SℚG_{S\mathbb{Q}} is the operation of universal torsion approximation. In the special case of chain complexes it corresponds to the derived functor that forms torsion subgroups (see the discussion at fracture theorem – Arithmetic fracturing of chain complexes).

Similarly, if one already looks at p-local spectra then torsion approximation is the homotopy fiber of Sℤ[p −1]S\mathbb{Z}[p^{-1}]-localization.

Definition

Let AA be an E-∞ ring and 𝔞⊂π 0A\mathfrak{a} \subset \pi_0 A a finitely generated ideal of its underlying commutative ring.

Definition

An AA-∞-module NN is an 𝔞\mathfrak{a}-torsion module if for all elements n∈π kNn \in \pi_k N and all elements a∈𝔞a \in \mathfrak{a} there is k∈ℕk \in \mathbb{N} such that a kn=0a^k n = 0.

(Lurie “Completions”, def. 4.1.3).

Proposition

The full sub-(∞,1)-category

AMod 𝔞tor↪AMod A Mod_{\mathfrak{a}tor} \hookrightarrow A Mod

is co-reflective and the co-reflector ʃ 𝔞&#643;_{\mathfrak{a}} – the torsion approximation – is smashing.

(Lurie “Completions”, prop. 4.1.12).

Proposition

For N∈AMod ≤0N \in A Mod_{\leq 0} then torsion approximation, prop. 1, intuced a monomorphism on π 0\pi_0

π 0ʃ 𝔞N↪π 0N \pi_0 &#643;_{\mathfrak{a}} N \hookrightarrow \pi_0 N

including the 𝔞\mathfrak{a}-nilpotent elements of π 0N\pi_0 N.

(Lurie “Completions”, prop. 4.1.18).

Relation to localization

Proposition

There is a natural homotopy fiber sequence

ʃ 𝔞⟶id⟶ʃ 𝔞dR &#643;_{\mathfrak{a}} \longrightarrow id \longrightarrow &#643;_{\mathfrak{a}dR}

relating 𝔞\mathfrak{a}-torsion approximation on the left with 𝔞\mathfrak{a}-localization on the right.

(Lurie “Completions”, remark 4.1.20)
