Idea

In string theory, D-branes appear in a “prequantum” version and in a more refined quantum version. A D-brane charge is an element in the twisted equivariant K-theory of spacetime, and the quantization of a prequantum D-brane given by a submanifold worldvolume carrying a Chan-Paton bundle is the push-forward of this equivariant vector bundle along the embedding. If the embedding is not into a fixed point of the orbifold action, then under this push-forward the D-brane charge is a sum of generating classes in twisted equivariant K-theory. Therefore the “quantum” D-branes corresponding to these generating classes of equivariant K-theory are called the fractional D-branes (as the others are “composed” of these). They can usually be obtained by quantization/push-forward of Chan-Paton gauge fields placed at orientifold fixed-points

More in detail, for XX a spacetime, possibly an orbifold, carrying a B-field which is modulated (as discussed there) by a map χ:X⟶B 2U(1)\chi \;\colon\; X \longrightarrow \mathbf{B}^2 U(1), then a prequantum boundary condition for the open string sigma-model charged under this B-field is a diagram of the form

Q ↙ ↘ i * ⇙ ξ X ↘ ↙ χ B 2U(1) ↓ ρ KUMod. \array{ && Q \\ & \swarrow && \searrow^{\mathrlap{i}} \\ \ast && \swArrow_\xi && X \\ & \searrow && \swarrow_{\chi} \\ && \mathbf{B}^2 U(1) \\ && \downarrow^{\mathrlap{\rho}} \\ && KU Mod } \,.

Typically here i:Q↪Xi \colon Q \hookrightarrow X is assumed to be a submanifold, or in the GG-orbifold case a GG-equivariant embedding of a GG-principal bundle on QQ (a map of the corresponding smooth stacks).

Here QQ is then the worldvolume of the prequantum D-brane, ξ\xi is the (underlying instanton sector of the) Chan-Paton gauge field that it carries.

If the Freed-Witten anomaly cancellation condition holds, which says that ii has K-orientation in χ\chi-twisted (and equivariant) complex K-theory, then this diagram has a motivic quantization to a map of KU-modules

KU •(*)⟶i !ξKU •+χ(X) KU^\bullet(\ast) \stackrel{i_! \xi}{\longrightarrow} KU^{\bullet + \chi}(X)

given by the push-forward/relative index of the Chan-Paton gauge field in topological K-theory from the worldvolume QQ to spacetime XX.

This is equivalent an element

ind i(ξ)∈KU •+χ(X) ind_i(\xi) \in KU^{\bullet + \chi}(X)

in the χ\chi-twisted K-theory of XX, and as such it is called the D-brane charge.

Not every element of the twisted K-theory may arise this way. We may think of a general morphism

q:KU •(*)⟶KU •+χ(X) q \;\colon\; KU^\bullet(\ast) \stackrel{}{\longrightarrow} KU^{\bullet + \chi}(X)

as a quantum boundary condition, hence as a “quantum” D-brane for the open string sigma-model. These include all elements of the twisted K-theory, in particular the generators. These generators are called the fractional D-branes.

In particular, in the simple case that X=ℂ n/ΓX = \mathbb{C}^n/\Gamma is the global orbifold of a finite group acting on a Cartesian space, the complex K-theory corresponds to the representation ring of Γ\Gamma. In this case the fractional branes correspond simply to the irreducible representations of Γ\Gamma.
