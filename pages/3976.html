Definition

The circle group 𝕋\mathbb{T} is equivalently (isomorphically)

  • the quotient group ℝ/ℤ\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} of the additive group of real numbers by the additive group of integers, induced by the canonical embedding ℤ↪ℝ\mathbb{Z} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R};

  • the unitary group U(1)\mathrm{U}(1);

  • the special orthogonal group SO(2)SO(2);

  • the subgroup of the group of units ℂ ×\mathbb{C}^\times of the field of complex numbers (its multiplicative group) given by those of any fixed positive modulus (standardly 11).

Properties

For general abstract properties usually the first characterization is the most important one. Notably it implies that the circle group fits into a short exact sequence

0→ℤ→ℝ→𝕋→0, 0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{T} \to 0 \,,

the “real exponential exact sequence”.

(On the other hand, the last characterization is usually preferred when one wants to be concrete.)

A character of an abelian group AA is simply a homomorphism from AA to the circle group.

U(1)U(1) is the compact real form of the multiplicative group 𝔾 m=ℂ ×\mathbb{G}_m = \mathbb{C}^\times over the complex numbers, see at form of an algebraic group – Circle group and multiplicative group.

Related concepts

  • A principal bundle with structure group the circle group is a circle bundle. The canonically corresponding associated bundle under the standard representation of U(1)↪ℂU(1) \hookrightarrow \mathbb{C} is a complex line bundle.
