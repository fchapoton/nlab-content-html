Idea

What is called self-conjugate K-theory of spaces XX (Anderson 64) is KR-theory on real spaces of the form X×S 0,2X \times S^{0,2}, where the second factor denotes the circle equipped with the antipodal ℤ 2\mathbb{Z}_2-action (see at real space for the notation).

Applications

In the context of type II string theory on orientifolds KSCKSC-theory is the cohomology theory in which the RR-fields of the I˜\tilde I-variant of type I superstring theory are cocycles (Witten 98, DMR 13, section 3.3.)
