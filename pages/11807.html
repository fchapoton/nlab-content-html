Idea

For R:𝒟→𝒞R \colon \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C} a functor which preserves finite limits then the left adjoint profunctor 𝒞→Func(𝒟,∞Grpd) op\mathcal{C}\to Func(\mathcal{D}, \infty Grpd)^{op} factors through the pro-objects Pro(𝒟)Pro(\mathcal{D}). This 𝒞→Pro(𝒟)\mathcal{C}\to Pro(\mathcal{D}) is the pro-left adjoint to RR. Its extension L:Pro(𝒞)→Pro(𝒟)L\colon Pro(\mathcal{C})\to Pro(\mathcal{D}) is a genuine left adjoint to Pro(R)Pro(R), the proadjoint.

Example

Pro-étale homotopy type

Consider any (∞,1)-sheaf ∞-topos with its global sections geometric morphism

H⟶Γ⟵Δ∞Grpd. \mathbf{H} \stackrel{\overset{\Delta}{\longleftarrow}}{\underset{\Gamma}{\longrightarrow}} \infty Grpd \,.

If here the constant ∞-stack inverse image Δ\Delta does have a further left adjoint (∞,1)-functor Π\Pi (so that H\mathbf{H} is a locally ∞-connected (∞,1)-topos) then Π\Pi may be understood as taking any object to its fundamental ∞-groupoid or geometric realization homotopy type.

In general Π\Pi does not exist, but the pro-left adjoint Π pro\Pi_{pro} of Δ\Delta may always be formed. This sends any object to what in the case of the ∞\infty-topos over an étale site is called its étale homotopy type.

In general Π pro\Pi_{pro} sends the terminal object to the shape of H\mathbf{H}.
