Idea

Given a Dirichlet character

χ:(ℤ/Nℤ) ×⟶ℂ × \chi \;\colon\; (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^\times \longrightarrow \mathbb{C}^\times

it is called even if χ(−1)=1\chi(-1) = 1 and odd if χ(−1)=−1\chi(-1) = -1.

The theta function associated with χ\chi is the function on ℝ +\mathbb{R}_+ defined by

θ χ(τ)≔{∑n∈ℤχ(n)exp(−πn 2t/N) ifχeven ∑n∈ℤnχ(n)exp(−πn 2t/N) ifχodd \theta_\chi(\tau) \coloneqq \left\{ \array{ \underset{n \in \mathbb{Z}}{\sum} \chi(n) \exp(-\pi n^2 t/N) & if\; \chi\; even \\ \underset{n \in \mathbb{Z}}{\sum} n \chi(n) \exp(-\pi n^2 t/N) & if\; \chi\; odd } \right.

where NN is the conductor of χ\chi.

Properties

Relation to Jacobi theta function

For trivial χ\chi the Dirichlet theta reproduces the Jacobi theta function.

Mellin transform and relation to Dirichlet L-function

The Mellin transform of this is the completed Dirichlet L-function associated with χ\chi (e.g. Continuations, p. 8)
