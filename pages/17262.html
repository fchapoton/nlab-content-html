Idea

The quaternion group of order 8, Q 8Q_8, is the finite subgroup of SU(2) Q 8⊂SU(2)≃S 3⊂ℍQ_8 \subset SU(2) \simeq S^3 \subset \mathbb{H} of unit quaternions which consists of the canonical four basis-quaternions and their negatives:

Q 8={±1,±i±j±k}. Q_8 \;=\; \big\{ \pm 1, \, \pm i \, \pm j \, \pm k \big\} \,.

This is isomorphic to the binary dihedral group of the same order Q 8≃2D 4Q_8 \simeq 2 D_4. As such, the Dynkin diagram that corresponds to Q 8Q_8 under the ADE-classification of finite subgroups of SU(2) is D4, the triality-invariant one.

This order-8 quaternion group Q 8Q_8 is the first in a row of generalized quaternion groups?, Q 4nQ_{4n}, which are also examples of dicyclic groups? which class forms part of an even larger family.

Definition

The generalised quaternion group of order 4n4n, n≥2n\geq 2, is the group Q 4nQ_{4n} defined by the presentation ⟨x,y|x 2n=x ny −2=y −1xyx=1⟩\langle x,y | x^{2n}= x^{n} y^{-2}=y^{-1}x y x=1\rangle. The quaternion group (of order 8) is Q 4nQ_{4n} for n=2n=2.

Properties

  • Q 4nQ_{4n} is (isomorphic to) a finite subgroup of ℍ *\mathbb{H}^\ast as can be seen by taking generators x=jx=j and y=cos(π/n)+isin(π/n)y=\cos(\pi/n) + i\sin(\pi/n). For n=2n=2 this simply yields the subgroup generated by ii and jj.

  • Q 4nQ_{4n} has another presentation as ⟨R,S,T|R 2=S 2=T n=RST⟩\langle R, S, T | R^2=S^2=T^n=R S T\rangle. RSTR S T as a power of each of the generators is central and Q 4n/⟨RST⟩=D 2nQ_{4n}/\langle R S T\rangle= D_{2n}, where D 2n=⟨R,S,T|R 2=S 2=T n=RST=1⟩D_{2n}=\langle R, S, T | R^2=S^2=T^n=R S T=1\rangle is the dihedral group of order 2n2n.

  • Q 8Q_8 is a Hamiltonian group i.e. a non-abelian group such that every subgroup is normal. Moreover, a general structure theorem for Hamiltonian groups by Baer (1933) says that every Hamiltonian group has a direct product group-decomposition containing Q 8Q_8 as a factor hence, in particular, every Hamiltonian group contains Q 8Q_8 as a subgroup! (cf. Scott (1987, p.253))

  • Q 8Q_8 is the multiplicative part of the quaternionic near-field J 9J_9. (cf. Weibel (2007))

Proposition

(inclusion of Q 8Q_8 into finite subgroups of SU(2))

Among the finite subgroups of SU(2) (hence among all “finite quaternion groups”) the quaternion group of order 8, Q 8Q_8 is a proper subgroup precisely of the three exceptional cases:

  • Q 8⊂2TQ_8 \subset 2 T the binary tetrahedral group (normal),

  • Q 8⊂2OQ_8 \subset 2 O the binary octahedral group (not normal)

  • Q 8⊂2IQ_8 \subset 2 I the binary icosahedral group (not normal)

(e.g. Koca-Moc-Koca 16, p. 8, pointing to Coxeter-Moser 65 and Coxeter 73)
