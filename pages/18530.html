Idea

The scaling degree or degree of divergence (Steinmann 71) or more generally the degree (Weinstein 78) of a distribution on Cartesian space ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n is a measure for how it behaves at the origin 0∈ℝ n0 \in \mathbb{R}^n under rescaling x↦λxx \mapsto \lambda x of the canonical coordinates.

The concept controls the problem of extension of distributions from the complement ℝ n∖{0}\mathbb{R}^n \setminus \{0\} of the origin to all of ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n. Such extensions are important notably in the construction of perturbative quantum field theories via causal perturbation theory, where the freedom in the choice of such extensions models the renormalization freedom (“counter-terms”) in the construction.

Definition

Definition

(rescaled distribution)

Let n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}. For λ∈(0,∞)⊂ℝ\lambda \in (0,\infty) \subset \mathbb{R} a positive real number write

ℝ n ⟶m λ ℝ n x ↦ λx \array{ \mathbb{R}^n &\overset{m_\lambda}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{R}^n \\ x &\mapsto& \lambda x }

for the diffeomorphism given by multiplication with λ\lambda, using the canonical real vector space-structure of ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n.

Then for u∈𝒟′(ℝ n)u \in \mathcal{D}'(\mathbb{R}^n) a distribution on the Cartesian space ℝ n\mathbb{R}^n the rescaled distribution is the pullback of uu along m λm_\lambda

u λ≔m λ *U∈𝒟′(ℝ n). u_\lambda \coloneqq m_\lambda^\ast U \;\in\; \mathcal{D}'(\mathbb{R}^n) \,.

Explicitly, this is given by

𝒟(ℝ n) ⟶⟨u λ,−⟩ ℝ b ↦ λ −n⟨u,b(λ −1⋅(−))⟩. \array{ \mathcal{D}(\mathbb{R}^n) &\overset{ \langle u_\lambda, - \rangle}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{R} \\ b &\mapsto& \lambda^{-n} \langle u , b(\lambda^{-1}\cdot (-))\rangle } \,.

Similarly for X⊂ℝ nX \subset \mathbb{R}^n an open subset which is invariant under m λm_\lambda, the rescaling of a distribution u∈𝒟′(X)u \in \mathcal{D}'(X) is is u λ≔m λ *uu_\lambda \coloneqq m_\lambda^\ast u.

Definition

(scaling degree of a distribution)

Let n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N} and let X⊂ℝ nX \subset \mathbb{R}^n be an open subset of Cartesian space which is invariant under m λm_\lambda (def. 1) for all λ∈(0,∞)\lambda \in (0,\infty), and let u∈𝒟′(X)u \in \mathcal{D}'(X) be a distribution on this subset.

The scaling degree of uu is the infimum

sd(u)≔inf{ω∈ℝ|limλ→0λ ωu λ=0} sd(u) \;\coloneqq\; inf \left\{ \omega \in \mathbb{R} \;\vert\; \underset{\lambda \to 0}{\lim} \lambda^\omega u_\lambda = 0 \right\}

of the set of real numbers ω\omega such that the limit of the rescaled distribution λ ωu λ\lambda^\omega u_\lambda (def. 1) vanishes. If there is no such ω\omega one sets sd(u)≔∞sd(u) \coloneqq \infty.

The degree of divergence of uu is the difference of the scaling degree by the dimension of the underlying space:

deg(u)≔sd(u)−n. deg(u) \coloneqq sd(u) - n \,.

Examples

Example

(scaling degree of ordinary functions)

If a distribution comes from an ordinary function b∈C ∞(X)⊂𝒟′(X)b \in C^\infty(X) \subset \mathcal{D}'(X), then its scaling degree (def. 2) is non-positive

sd(b)≤0. sd(b) \leq 0 \,.

Concretely, if the first non-vanishing derivative D kb(0)D^k b(0) of bb at 0 occurs as k∈ℕk \in \mathbb{N}, then the scaling degree is −k-k.

Example

(scaling degree of derivatives of delta-distibutions)

Let α∈ℕ n\alpha \in \mathbb{N}^n be a multi-index and δ α∈𝒟′(X)\delta^\alpha \in \mathcal{D}'(X) the corresponding derivatives of the delta distribution δ(0)\delta(0) supported at 00. Then the degree of divergence (def. 2) is the total degree the derivatives

deg(δ (α)(0))=|α|. deg( \delta^{(\alpha)}(0)) = {\vert \alpha \vert} \,.
Example

Let

E F(x)=1(2π) n∫ ℝ ne ip⋅xp 2−m 2+i0d np E_F(x) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{(2\pi)^n}\int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \frac{e^{i p\cdot x}}{p^2 - m^2 + i 0} d^n p

be the Feynman propagator for the massive free scalar field on nn-dimensional Minkowski spacetime (a Green function for the Klein-Gordon equation). Its scaling degree is

sd(E F)=n−2. sd(E_F) = n - 2 \,.

(Brunetti-Fredenhagen 00, example 3 on p. 22)

Properties

Proposition

Let X⊂ℝ nX \subset \mathbb{R}^n and u∈𝒟′(X)u \in \mathcal{D}'(X) be a distribution as in def. 1, such that its scaling degree is finite sd(u)<∞sd(u) \lt \infty (def. 2).

  1. sd(u⊗v)≤sd(u)+sd(v)sd(u \otimes v) \leq sd(u) + sd(v) for v∈𝒟′(Y)v \in \mathcal{D}'(Y) another distribution on Y⊂ℝ n′Y \subset \mathbb{R}^{n'};

  2. deg(fu)≤deg(u)−kdeg(f u) \leq deg(u) - k for f∈C ∞(X)f \in C^\infty(X) and f (α)(0)=0f^{(\alpha)}(0) = 0 for |α|≤k−1{\vert \alpha\vert} \leq k-1;

  3. deg(∂ αu)≤deg(u)+|α|deg(\partial_\alpha u) \leq deg(u) + {\vert \alpha\vert};

  4. deg(x αu)≤deg(u)−|α|deg(x^\alpha u) \leq deg(u) - {\vert \alpha\vert}.

Here α∈ℕ n\alpha \in \mathbb{N}^n a multi-index.

(Brunetti-Fredenhagen 00, lemma 5.1)
