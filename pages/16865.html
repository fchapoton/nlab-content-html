Idea

The Gaeta topos construction assigns a Grothendieck topos to a small extensive category.

The construction arises in attempts (Lawvere 1976, 1991) to build a gros topos of spaces with an inherent space-quantity ‘duality’. The terminology is apparently a tribute to the Spanish algebraic geometer Federico Gaeta? who in his lecture notes of A. Grothendieck's Buffalo 1973 colloquium (Gaeta 1974) has pointed out the utility of the extensivity property for functorial algebraic geometry.1

Definition

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a small finitary extensive category. The Gaeta topology2 on 𝒞\mathcal{C} is the Grothendieck topology J GJ_G generated by all finite families (X i→X)(X_i\to X) such that ∑ iX i→X\sum_i X_i\to X is an isomorphism. The Gaeta topos G(𝒞)G(\mathcal{C}) is the topos of sheaves for the site (𝒞,J G)(\mathcal{C}, J_G).

The next proposition shows that G(𝒞)G(\mathcal{C}) is not only a topos but a multisorted variety as well as it coincides with the category of finite product preserving presheaves on 𝒞\mathcal{C}:

Proposition

Let 𝒞 op\mathcal{C}^{op} be a small Cauchy complete category with finite products. The category Prod(𝒞 op,Set)Prod(\mathcal{C}^{op},Set) of finite product preserving functors 𝒞 op→Set\mathcal{C}^{op}\to Set is a topos iff 𝒞\mathcal{C} is extensive. Moreover, in this case Prod(𝒞 op,Set)Prod(\mathcal{C}^{op},Set) coincides with G(𝒞)G(\mathcal{C}).

Cf. Carboni-Pedicchio-Rosicky (2001, p.72) and Lawvere (2008, p.498).

From an algebraic perspective, Prod(𝒞 op,Set)Prod(\mathcal{C}^{op},Set) is the category 𝒞 op−Mod\mathcal{C}^{op}-Mod of algebras of the multisorted algebraic theory 𝒞 op\mathcal{C}^{op} .

Note, since they are naturally equivalent to Hom 𝒞(−,X)Hom_\mathcal{C}(-,X) which preserve all limits (cf. hom-functor), representable presheaves preserve finite products, in particular, and hence are sheaves for J GJ_G; in other words, the Gaeta topology is subcanonical.
