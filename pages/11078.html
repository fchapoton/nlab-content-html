Idea

An (∞,1)-category is nn-semiadditive for n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N} if (∞,1)-colimits over certain diagrams coincide with their (∞,1)-limits in a canonical way (see Hopkins-Lurie 14, def. 4.4.2). Here for n=0n=0 the condition is that coproducts coincide with products, hence that bilimits exist.

So an (∞,1)-category that happens to be an ordinary category is 0-semiadditive precisely if it is a semiadditive category in the traditional sense. Generally, an (∞,1)-category is 0-semiadditive precisely if its homotopy category is semiadditive (Hopkins-Lurie 14, remark 4.4.13).

Examples

  • For RR an A-∞ ring such that its connected component ring π 0(A)\pi_0(A) is a vector space over the rational numbers, then the (∞,1)-category of modules AModA Mod is nn-semiadditive for all n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N} (Hopkins-Lurie 14, example 4.4.22).
