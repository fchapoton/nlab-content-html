Semi-left-exact reflections

Semi-left-exact reflections

Idea

A semi-left-exact reflection is a reflector into a reflective subcategory that preserves some pullbacks. It fits into a hierarchy of left-exactness properties for reflectors:

left exact? $\Rightarrow$ stable units? $\Rightarrow$ **semi-left-exact** $\Rightarrow$ simple

In particular, semi-left-exactness is sufficient to imply that the corresponding reflective factorization system exists and can be constructed in one step, and that the reflective subcategory inherits local cartesian closure from the ambient category.

Definitions

Let CC be a category with finite limits, and B⊆CB\subseteq C a full reflective subcategory with reflector L:C→BL:C\to B. Let EE be the class of morphisms inverted by LL, and let MM be the class of morphisms right orthogonal to EE, i.e. (E,M)(E,M) is the associated reflective prefactorization system.

The following is a combination of Theorems 4.1 and 4.3 from CHK with Proposition 1.3 from GK.

Theorem

The following are equivalent:

  1. Every pullback of an EE-morphism along an MM-morphism is an EE-morphism.
  2. LL preserves pullbacks along MM-morphisms.
  3. LL preserves pullbacks along any morphism in BB.
  4. If f:x→yf:x\to y is a morphism in BB, then f *:C/y→C/xf^* : C/y \to C/x preserves EE-morphisms.
  5. (If CC is locally cartesian closed) If f:x→yf:x\to y is a morphism in BB, then f *:C/x→C/yf_* : C/x \to C/y maps B/xB/x into B/yB/y.

Proof

Since all morphisms in BB are MM-morphisms, (2) implies (3). Now given (3), the functor f *f^* commutes with localization L/x:C/x→B/xL/x : C/x \to B/x and L/y:C/y→B/yL/y : C/y \to B/y, so if e∈Ee\in E is inverted by the latter then f *(e)f^*(e) is inverted by the former; thus (4) holds. On the other hand, f *(e)f^*(e) is also the pullback of ee along a pullback of ff, and since the latter is an MM-morphism, (1) also implies (4).

It now follows that if f∈Mf\in M to start with, then λ f\lambda_f must be an isomorphism, hence the naturality square for η\eta at ff is a pullback. Now the pullback pasting law combined with (4) implies (1) and (2).

Finally, if CC is locally cartesian closed, then (4) implies (5) by adjointness, while (5) implies (3) in the form L/x∘f *≅L/y∘f *L/x \circ f^* \cong L/y \circ f^* by passage to right-adjoint mates.

Of course, if LL preserves all pullbacks (i.e. it is left exact?), then it is semi-left-exact. The statement about the existence of the reflective factorization system implies that any semi-left-exact reflection is simple.

Properties

Semi-left-exactness of a reflection ℓ\ell of CC into B⊆CB\subseteq C is also equivalent to saying that for any x∈Cx\in C, the right adjoint of the induced functor ℓ:C/x→B/ℓ(x)\ell\colon C/x \to B/\ell(x) (which is given by pullback along η x\eta_x) is fully faithful. In this form it is equivalent to (a particular case of) the notion of admissible reflection in categorical Galois theory.
